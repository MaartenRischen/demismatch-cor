A human being, in plain words.

No jargon, no blame. Two questions, answered straight.

How humans work

You are not a machine and not a blank slate. You are an organism, shaped over millions of years to survive and raise the next generation. Everything you feel - hunger, fear, loneliness, drive, joy - is a signal from a system doing its job. Those signals are information about what is happening inside you and around you. They are not verdicts on reality, and they are not verdicts on your worth.

You are not one mind making one decision. You are many specialized systems running at once. One watches for danger. One keeps you close to the people you trust. One tracks where you stand in the group. Others run hunger, sleep, movement, and the urge to chase something worth having. Each was tuned for the world your ancestors lived in: small groups, familiar faces, light and dark, real effort with real consequences.

That world is gone. The systems are not. They run their old settings against a world they were never built for, and they lean toward caution - a false alarm cost your ancestors little, a missed real threat could cost everything. So when something is off, they signal hard. The anxiety, the low mood, the restlessness, the loneliness: that is a system working exactly as designed, accurately reading a mismatch between what it expects and what the modern world gives it. Nothing in you is broken. The signal is real. The mismatch is the problem.

What humans need

A need is not a want. It is the specific input a system requires to settle. Your systems are asking for a short, stubborn list: people who are actually present and trusted, an environment that is not a constant low hum of threat, days that move with light and sleep and movement and real food, a place in a group small enough that you matter in it, and something worth pursuing that gives you real traction when you act.

These needs do not negotiate, and they do not trade. A full feed will not replace a friend. A feed full of strangers will not fill the place built for a few people who know you. The deepest ones you cannot think your way out of - you can override hunger or fear for a while, at a cost to your body, but you cannot reason yourself into not needing them.

And you do not need to live like your ancestors to meet them. You need the world around you to deliver what theirs delivered - connection, safety, agency, consequence - in whatever modern form does the job. Get those inputs right and the signals quiet on their own, because they were never the malfunction. They were the alarm. The fix is the environment that sets it off, not the person hearing it.
