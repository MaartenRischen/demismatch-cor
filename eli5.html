The Tiny Version

What Cor is trying to say.

Short version

If a plant droops in a dark closet, you do not call the plant defective. You change the light, water, soil, and temperature. Cor says humans work more like that than we admit: a lot of suffering is the organism reporting that its inputs are wrong.

Humans are organisms

We are not just floating minds. We are bodies that need daylight, sleep, movement, touch, belonging, play, purpose, safety, and real social connection.

Feelings are often alarms

Loneliness, anxiety, grief, shame, and restless craving are not always proof that something is wrong inside the person. Often they are signals about the life the person is currently living inside.

Modern life sells fast substitutes

Many systems offer the feeling of relief or reward without the real thing underneath. Cor calls these substitutes proxies: they can trigger the circuit without actually meeting the need.

What Cor Is

A map of human needs and human signals.

An atlas

Cor calls itself an atlas because it is trying to map the terrain. It wants to show the main human systems, what inputs they expect, and what happens when those inputs are missing.

Built from research

The site pulls together work from evolutionary biology, affective neuroscience, developmental psychology, anthropology, behavioral ecology, and related fields. The point is to make the chain of evidence visible.

Public and inspectable

This is not a black-box product. The site exposes the papers, the researchers, the cases, the gaps, and the first worked evaluation layer in public.

Atlas now, specification next

The project says clearly that the atlas exists now. The fuller machine-readable specification and test protocols are the next layer being built on top of it.

What Cor is not saying

  • It is not saying every painful feeling is healthy or that every problem is caused by mismatch.
  • It is not saying therapy, medicine, or crisis care are bad or unnecessary.
  • It is not saying people should just go live in a forest.
  • It is saying we often blame the individual too quickly and ignore the environment that person is trying to survive inside.
Why AI Matters

Because AI is getting very good at giving us what looks like what we want.

Many AI systems are trained to satisfy preferences: what people click, choose, rate highly, come back to, or ask for. Cor's warning is simple: in a mismatched world, those signals can point toward a substitute instead of the real need.

If someone is lonely, a system can give them endless simulated warmth. If someone wants status, a system can give them endless comparison. If someone wants comfort, a system can give them a highly optimized proxy. The user may feel immediate relief, but the deeper need can stay unresolved or even get worse.

That is why Cor cares so much about AI. A machine that does not know the difference between real resolution and a sticky proxy can become the most efficient deliverer of the wrong thing ever built.

Real need: connection

Proxy risk: an AI relationship that feels intimate but cannot actually share your life, body, or fate.

Real need: stable status

Proxy risk: infinite feeds that keep the status system comparing upward forever.

Real need: healthy development

Proxy risk: childhood environments tuned for retention instead of the inputs a developing human system really needs.

How Cor Thinks

The basic move is simple.

  1. Name the human system that is firing. Is this about attachment, status, grief, play, threat, care, movement, sleep, or something else?
  2. Ask what that system was built to get. What would actually help it settle or resolve?
  3. Check for proxy versus real input. Is the person getting the real thing, or only a cue that imitates it?
  4. Change the environment when possible. Instead of only telling the person to think differently, change the conditions the organism is reading.

One-sentence takeaway

Cor is trying to do one thing before AI and other powerful systems get much bigger: put a better picture of the human into the tools and environments that are shaping human life.
