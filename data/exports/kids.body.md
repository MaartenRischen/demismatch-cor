## How to read this document

Hi there! This is a friendly guide to how your body and brain work.

Here is the big idea. A long, long time ago, our bodies learned how to keep us safe and happy. They learned this for a world with lots of trees, animals, and close friends. But the world today is very different! It has phones, bright lights, and lots of fast food. So sometimes our feelings work just fine, but the world around us is new and tricky. When you feel bad, it does not mean something is broken inside you. Your body might just be sending a true signal that something around you does not fit.

Think of your feelings like little messages. A message that says "I am scared" or "I am lonely" is telling you something real. We should listen to these messages, not just turn them off.

Your body has many helpers inside it. Each helper has one special job. One watches for danger. One helps you make friends. One helps you sleep. They all work together, like players on a team.

### What is a "pretend version"?

Here is the most important trick to know. Each helper inside you looks for a special clue. When it sees the clue, it turns on and feels a certain way.

But sometimes a pretend version can show the same clue! It tricks the helper into turning on. The problem is, the pretend version does not give your body what it really needs. It is like a picture of a cookie. It looks like a cookie, but it will never make your tummy full.

So in this guide, for each helper, we will talk about the real thing and the pretend version that can trick your brain into feeling like it counts when it really does not.

## M1 - Threat Management

This helper watches for danger. When it sees something scary, it makes you freeze, fight, or run away. That keeps you safe! It feels better once you are safe and the danger is gone.

The real thing is danger you can run from, like a barking dog you can step away from. Then it is over, and you calm down. The pretend version is scary stuff on a screen or news that makes you upset on purpose. It turns on your alarm, but there is nothing real you can run from. So the alarm just keeps ringing and never turns off, like a smoke alarm beeping when there is no fire.

## M2 - Pursuit / Exploration

This helper makes you want to explore, try things, and reach goals. It feels happy when you work hard at something real and finish it, like building a fort or learning to ride a bike.

The real thing is doing a real task and getting a real result you can see and touch. The pretend version is apps or games that give little prizes over and over to keep you tapping. They make you want more and more, but you never really feel finished. It is like chasing a balloon that floats just out of reach and never lands.

## M3 - Social Bonding

This helper makes you love and need other people. It wants you to be close to people you trust, like your family and best friends. When someone you love is far away or gone, it can hurt a lot. That hurt is real, and it shows how much your bonds matter.

The real thing is being together in person with people who care about you and care back. The pretend version is something like a computer friend made to act like it loves you. It can feel like a friend, but it cannot truly care for you the way a real person can. A warm hug from a friend is real. A picture waving on a screen is not the same.

## M4 - Social Calibration / Play

This helper learns how to get along with others by playing. Running around, chasing, and making up games teaches you the rules of being with friends. Animals that are scared or sick do not play, so play is a sign things are okay.

The real thing is playing with other kids your age, making up your own games with your bodies, not just watching. The pretend version is a game you play alone on a screen with no real friend to play back. It can feel fun, but it does not teach you how to play with people. It is like dancing alone instead of dancing with a partner.

## M5 - Status Monitoring

This helper notices where you fit with the people around you. It makes you feel proud when you do well and shy when you slip up. It is always quietly working, like a little checker in the background.

The real thing is being respected by a small group of people who really know you, like your class or your team. The pretend version is comparing yourself to millions of people online by counting likes and followers. There are too many people to really know! It can make you feel small for no good reason, like trying to win a race against the whole world at once.

## M6 - Controllability / Agency

This helper checks if your actions make a difference. When you try something and it works, you learn that you can change things. This is very important, because it helps you keep going instead of giving up.

The real thing is doing something where you can see your effort matter, like planting a seed and watching it grow. The pretend version is a game or machine that makes you feel busy and in charge, but nothing real ever happens. It tricks you into feeling like you are doing something when you are not, like pretending to drive a car that is not really moving.

## M7 - Circadian Regulation

This helper is like a master clock inside you. It uses light and dark to set the right time for sleeping, waking, eating, and thinking. It keeps everything running on schedule.

The real thing is bright daylight in the day and a dark, quiet space at night. That helps your clock stay correct. The pretend version is bright screens at night that fool your clock into thinking it is daytime. Then your body gets confused about when to sleep. It is like a clock that points to the wrong hour, so everyone shows up at the wrong time.

## M8 - Immune Regulation

This helper fights off germs when you get sick. It makes you feel tired and want to rest so your body can heal. That is why you feel like staying in bed with a cold.

The real thing is catching a bug, fighting it off, and getting all better. Then the tired feeling goes away. The trouble starts when stress, poor sleep, or being lonely keep this helper switched on a little bit all the time, even with no germ to fight. The "off" switch gets worn out, like a faucet that drips and drips and will not fully close.

## M9 - Care / Alloparenting

This helper helps grown-ups take care of children. Long ago, lots of people helped raise each child, not just one or two. Your body expects a whole village of helpers, not just one tired parent.

There are two different things to know here. One is about how many caring helpers there are. The real thing is a big group of trusted people who help. The least helpful is one parent doing it all alone with no one to help. That is not a trick, it is just an empty shelf, missing what is needed.

The other thing is a fake helper, like a screen or a robot babysitter. It can keep a child busy, but it cannot truly care the way a real person does.

## M10 - Movement / Regulatory

This helper turns on when your body moves. Moving is not just exercise. It helps your whole body fix and clean itself, and it helps your mood too! Long ago, people walked many miles every day just by living their lives.

The real thing is moving all day long as part of normal life, like walking, carrying, and playing. The closest helper after that is a job where you move a lot. The hardest is sitting still almost all day. One gym visit can help a little, but it does not match a whole day of moving. It is like watering a plant once a week instead of giving it a little water every day.

## M11 - Cooperation / Alliance

This helper helps you work together with others, share fairly, and notice when someone is not being fair. It likes being part of a group where everyone knows each other and keeps their promises.

The real thing is a group of people who trust each other, small enough that everyone remembers who is kind and who is not. When groups get very big, we need rules and helpers to keep things fair, and that still works okay. The pretend version is trading with total strangers you will never see again, or pretend friends that have no name and no story to share. There is no trust to hold it together, like a game where no one remembers the rules.

## M12 - Contamination Avoidance

This helper is your disgust feeling. It keeps you away from rotten food and germs that could make you sick. That "yuck!" feeling is actually trying to protect you.

The real thing is feeling yucky about real germs, like spoiled food, so you stay away. Sometimes this helper is extra careful and says "yuck" to safe things too, just to be sure. There is also an idea that this disgust feeling can spread to how we judge other people, but scientists are still less sure about that part. The simple, well-known job is keeping you away from germs, like a guard who keeps bad stuff out.

## M13 - Energy Regulation

This helper handles hunger and feeling full. It tells you when to eat and when to stop. It is good at saving and sharing your energy where your body needs it.

The real thing is eating whole foods that take some work to chew and that change with the seasons. These help you feel full at the right time. The pretend version is super-processed snacks made to taste amazing so you keep eating and eating. They break the "I am full" signal, so your tummy cannot tell you to stop. It is like a stop sign that someone covered up, so you keep going past it.

## M14 - Reproductive Motivation

This is a grown-up helper. It has three parts that handle wanting to be close, feeling drawn to someone special, and staying bonded together as partners. It shapes a lot of how grown-ups feel and act.

The real thing is a real partner you share life with. The pretend versions are things on screens that show the clues of a partner but are not a real person who shares your life. They can switch the helper on, but they cannot give the real closeness and bond that really fill the need.

## R1 - Touch

This one is a little different. It is not a helper that pushes you to do something. It is more like a special feeling channel that helps your bonding helper (M3) feel calm and safe. Your skin has tiny nerves that love gentle, friendly touch.

The real thing is a warm hug or hand-hold from someone you trust who hugs you back. Other kinds of touch, like a soft blanket or a touch machine, can feel nice, but they are not a living friend. A real cuddle from someone who loves you does something special that a blanket cannot. Gentle touch matters so much that holding tiny new babies skin-to-skin helps keep them safe and healthy.
