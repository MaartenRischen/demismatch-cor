## How to read this document

Here is the central idea of this framework, stated plainly: a great deal of modern human suffering is not a sign that something is broken inside you. It is the sound of a well-built alarm system going off in a world it was never designed for.

Your mind and body are full of ancient machinery, shaped over millions of years to keep your ancestors alive and able to raise children. That machinery produces feelings - hunger, fear, loneliness, the itch to chase a goal, the sting of low status. These feelings are signals. By default, this framework treats them as honest information about your circumstances, not as glitches to be silenced. (That default can be set aside when there is real evidence of a different cause - for example, illness, sleep loss, hormonal shifts, injury, or the effects of a drug or medication.)

The catch is that these systems were tuned for a world of small face-to-face groups, physical work, real food, and constant closeness. Modern life often pushes them outside the range they expect. The signal is still accurate. The world has changed underneath it.

### The derivation stack

This framework is built in layers, each one forced by the layer beneath it:

- **Frame:** We never see the world directly. We see it through evolved interfaces built to keep us alive, not to show us the unvarnished truth. And the feelings these interfaces produce are treated, by default, as meaningful information.
- **Premise:** Evolution's only scorecard is inclusive fitness - surviving and reproducing. To serve that, the organism contains many specialized, interacting subsystems. Modern environments routinely push them out of range.
- **Properties:** From this it follows that many painful feelings are intelligent defensive signals, not defects; that humans regulate themselves through their social bonds; that trouble in one system spreads to others; that systems can be switched on by fake cues without being switched off; that they tend to over-react when the cost of missing a real danger was high; and that they compete, take priority over one another, and get calibrated by early experience.
- **Consequences:** Unresolved activation builds up as wear and tear on the body. Markets learn to sell fake fixes. And because forcing yourself to override these systems with willpower is exhausting and unreliable, changing your actual environment is usually the more durable fix.

### How to read a proxy gradient

The most important tool in this document is the **proxy gradient**. A proxy is a stand-in - something that imitates the real thing.

Here is the key insight, and it holds for every gradient: **the cue stays exactly the same at every level. Only whether your real need gets met changes.** The urge feels identical whether you are doing the real thing or a hollow imitation. What changes is whether anything actually satisfies the system and lets it switch off.

Each gradient is a ladder. At the top is the real thing, which fully meets the need. As you descend, more of the real satisfaction is stripped away while the urge keeps firing. At the bottom is a pure proxy: it fires the cue perfectly and supplies nothing. The system never gets the signal that it can stand down - an "open loop" that just keeps running.

A note on evidence: some rungs are well-evidenced, some are derived from the architecture, and a few are flagged as contested or speculative. We say so as we go.

## M1 - Threat Management

### What it does

This is your danger-detection and defense system. When it spots a threat, it makes you freeze, fight, or flee. It also includes an offensive mode - the frustration-and-aggression response. It runs on the oldest, deepest parts of the brain (the amygdala, periaqueductal gray, and hypothalamus), which act faster than conscious thought and can override it.

### What actually satisfies it

Safety. The threat is eliminated, escaped, or avoided, and the system can switch off.

### The proxy gradient

The cue is the alarm itself. What changes down the ladder is whether you can actually resolve the danger:

- **FULL (need met):** acute danger you can fight or flee from, and then escape. The alarm fires, you act, the threat ends, the system stands down.
- **PARTIAL (partly resolved):** a real, present threat that a trusted other helps you resolve.
- **MIXED (partly resolved):** a chronic, slow-burning real threat with no action that ends it.
- **MINIMAL (fires, no resolution):** an abstract or anticipated threat with no physical target to fight or flee.
- **ZERO (fires, no resolution):** engineered outrage or threat media designed to keep the alarm ringing. (This bottom rung is flagged as contestable.)

### What goes wrong in modern life

Modern threats - debt, a hostile email, social media pile-ons, status anxiety - cannot be punched or outrun. There is no moment of escape, so the alarm never gets its "all clear." The system simply stays on.

### How well-evidenced

This is among the most strongly supported mechanisms here, with 115 evidence links. It is "forced" - the framework could not be built without it. Key researchers: Panksepp, LeDoux, Mobbs. The system is about 500 million years old, shared by all vertebrates. When it activates strongly, it suppresses many other systems (pursuit, play, reproduction, movement, social calibration); the only thing that overrides it is the social bonding system.

## M2 - Pursuit / Exploration

### What it does

This is your general-purpose seeking drive - the engine that pushes you to explore, chase goals, build skills, and engage with the world. (Sexual motivation is handled separately, by M14.)

### What actually satisfies it

Engagement with real challenges that produce tangible outcomes: goal progress, skills gained, resources acquired. Crucially, satisfaction requires both the chase and a real result that closes the loop.

### The proxy gradient

The cue here is "wanting" - the pull to pursue, driven by the brain chemical dopamine. It feels the same regardless. What changes is whether a real outcome arrives to close the loop:

- **FULL (need met):** engaging a real challenge that yields a real outcome.
- **PARTIAL (partly resolved):** knowledge work with a real but abstracted, delayed outcome.
- **MIXED (partly resolved):** a variable-reward app or feed that pays off for real only intermittently.
- **MINIMAL (fires, no resolution):** infinite novelty - endless content scrolling or idle browsing.
- **ZERO (fires, no resolution):** a machine gambling product engineered to keep you playing, with a guaranteed net loss.

### What goes wrong in modern life

Variable-reward apps are designed to fire the seeking drive again and again without ever delivering the real outcome that would let it rest. The loop stays open: you keep wanting, nothing satisfies.

### How well-evidenced

Forced, 67 evidence links. Top and bottom rungs are well-evidenced; the trickiest call is whether the variable-reward app really sits above mindless scrolling. Key researchers: Panksepp, Berridge. About 500 million years old. It gets suppressed by threat, social bonding, circadian, immune, and energy systems.

## M3 - Social Bonding

### What it does

This is your attachment and co-regulation system - the machinery that forms bonds with other people and lets them help steady your nervous system. It also carries the grief response. It has two states: security, which broadly enables you, and grief, which broadly shuts you down.

### What actually satisfies it

Reliable co-regulation through physical presence, arranged in nested layers: roughly 5 intimate co-regulators, about 15 in a sympathy group, about 50 close partners, and about 150 stable face-to-face relationships - the "Dunbar number," the outer limit of how many real relationships a human can track. The innermost ~5 do most of the regulating, and what matters most there is reliability, not headcount.

### The proxy gradient

The cue is the same pull toward connection. What changes is how much real co-regulatory function survives:

- **FULL (need met):** a reliable, physically present inner circle.
- **PARTIAL (partly resolved):** a real bonded inner circle, but held at a distance through remote contact.
- **MIXED (partly resolved):** a large mediated network that only partly routes back to real physical presence.
- **MINIMAL (fires, no resolution):** one-sided parasocial attachment to a figure who does not know you exist.
- **ZERO (fires, no resolution):** an AI companion engineered to simulate a two-way bond. (This rung is theoretically evidenced.)

Note: being isolated or living alone is not a rung on this ladder. It is "empty-shelf" deprivation - the need simply goes unmet.

### What goes wrong in modern life

The nuclear family, solo living, and screens substituting for physical presence all strip away the in-person co-regulation the system expects.

### How well-evidenced

This is the most heavily supported mechanism in the framework, with 192 evidence links. Forced. Key researchers: Bowlby, Panksepp, Coan, Hrdy. About 200 million years old. The supporting research includes a finding that strong social relationships carry roughly a 50% survival advantage. In its secure state it enables most other systems; nothing recorded suppresses it.

## M4 - Social Calibration / Play

### What it does

This is the play system. Through rough-and-tumble play, it teaches social rules and skills. It goes quiet in animals that are threatened or ill - play is a sign that conditions are safe.

### What actually satisfies it

Age-matched, self-directed, unsupervised, physical play with peers.

### The proxy gradient

The cue is the social-joy pull toward play. It still fires (in weaker or substitute form) even at the lower rungs. What changes is how much real, embodied, two-way play with a real partner survives:

- **FULL (need met):** age-matched, self-directed, unsupervised physical play with peers.
- **PARTIAL (partly resolved):** adult-structured, supervised physical activity, like organized sports.
- **MIXED (partly resolved):** screen-mediated multiplayer play with real peers.
- **MINIMAL (fires, no resolution):** solitary single-player screen play, with no reciprocating partner.
- **ZERO (fires, no resolution):** an engineered companion-AI or variable-reward "play" product. (Derived from the architecture.)

### What goes wrong in modern life

Structured activities crowd out free play. Screens replace bodies. The result is play deprivation - the system fires but never gets the real embodied reciprocity it needs.

### How well-evidenced

Forced, 55 evidence links. The most debatable call is ranking screen-play-with-real-peers above a solitary scripted game. Key researchers: Panksepp, Pellis & Pellis, Gray. About 200 million years old, present in mammals but absent in reptiles. It is suppressed by threat and by grief.

## M5 - Status Monitoring

### What it does

This system tracks where you stand relative to others - generating shame, pride, submission, the feeling of defeat, and a constant low-level vigilance about rank. It is not optional; it runs all the time.

### What actually satisfies it

An acceptable position within a reference group of about 150 people. Earned respect ("prestige") satisfies it better than respect won through intimidation ("dominance").

### The proxy gradient

The cue is the constant comparison signal. What changes is whether you have real standing in a real, knowable group:

- **FULL (need met):** earned prestige in a small face-to-face group.
- **PARTIAL (partly resolved):** a coerced or unstable dominance rank in a real group.
- **MIXED (partly resolved):** status-striving inside a large, impersonal society.
- **MINIMAL (fires, no resolution):** global mediated comparison by follower or like counts.
- **ZERO (fires, no resolution):** purchased status signals, like luxury-brand display. (The bottom two rungs are derived from the architecture.)

### What goes wrong in modern life

Social media throws you into global comparison against millions of "peers." There is always someone higher, which can trigger a status-defeat cascade that drags down other systems.

### How well-evidenced

Forced, 88 evidence links. The most contested point is whether purchased signals really sit below social-media comparison. Key researchers: Gilbert, Price, Mazur, Sapolsky, Marmot. About 50 to 100 million years old, found in primates and social mammals.

## M6 - Controllability / Agency

### What it does

This system tracks whether your actions actually produce results. Strikingly, its default setting is helplessness - a sense of agency has to be actively built and maintained through experience. It runs on a brain circuit linking the vmPFC and the dorsal raphe nucleus, and it is critical for breaking cascades, because regaining a sense of control can stop a downward spiral.

### What actually satisfies it

Genuine action-outcome feedback - seeing that your efforts register - and real decision latitude.

### The proxy gradient

The cue here is the detection of a link between what you do and what happens. What changes is whether you have real causal power over outcomes that matter:

- **FULL (need met):** decision latitude over consequential outcomes that visibly respond to your effort.
- **PARTIAL (partly resolved):** high demands but with reduced, still-real decision latitude.
- **MIXED (partly resolved):** abstract, surveilled, or screen-mediated work where your effort does not visibly register.
- **MINIMAL (fires, no resolution):** a chronically uncontrollable environment - learned helplessness.
- **ZERO (fires, no resolution):** an engineered simulated-agency loop (a slot machine, game, or dashboard) that fires the contingency cue while delivering zero real consequence. (Derived from the architecture.)

### What goes wrong in modern life

Abstract work where effort never visibly registers, rigid hierarchies, and bureaucratic systems all withdraw real control - while some products fake it.

### How well-evidenced

Forced, 38 evidence links. The first four rungs are well-evidenced; the trickiest call is whether true helplessness or a faked-control loop sits at the floor. Key researchers: Maier & Seligman. About 200 million years old. Suppressed by threat, reproduction, and social bonding.

## M7 - Circadian Regulation

### What it does

This is your body's master clock, the SCN, which synchronizes every system to the daily light-dark cycle. It gates metabolism, immunity, thinking, and mood. The modern problem is usually about the timing and quality of sleep, not its raw duration.

### What actually satisfies it

A dark-cued sleep onset, bright daylight during the day, and a consistent rhythm.

### The proxy gradient

This gradient works a little differently. Rather than a fake cue being fired, the real entraining signal - light - is steadily degraded and finally inverted:

- **FULL (need met):** the outdoor light-dark cycle, with sleep cued by darkness.
- **ATTENUATED (need met):** indoor daytime light with a mostly dark, dimly lit night.
- **PARTIAL (partly resolved):** a correctly set clock overridden by a misaligned social schedule - "social jetlag."
- **MINIMAL (fires, no resolution):** shift work and always-on connectivity, with the dark cue largely gone.
- **DEFEATED (fires, no resolution):** evening blue-light screens actively mis-setting the clock, giving false daylight at biological night.

### What goes wrong in modern life

Electric light, blue screens, shift work, and caffeine all disrupt the clock's signal.

### How well-evidenced

Forced, 20 evidence links - all five rungs are evidenced. The most debatable points are whether social jetlag or shift work is worse, and whether screens truly sit at the floor. Key researchers: Walker, Roenneberg, Czeisler. About 3 billion years old, reaching back to cyanobacteria.

## M8 - Immune Regulation

### What it does

This is the system behind "sickness behavior" - the cytokine signaling that, when you are fighting an infection, makes you withdraw, rest, and lose interest in everything else. It can override your conscious intentions, and it is a hub that connects inflammation to depression through cross-system cascades.

### What actually satisfies it

The pathogen is cleared and inflammation resolves.

### The proxy gradient

Here the trigger drifts away from a real, clearable pathogen toward lifestyle and psychosocial signals with nothing to clear - and the inflammatory off-switch itself wears down:

- **FULL (need met):** an acute infection or injury fought off and fully resolved.
- **ATTENUATED (need met):** recurrent acute stress that resolves each time but with incomplete recovery.
- **PARTIAL (partly resolved):** sustained lifestyle drivers (a sedentary life, poor diet, sleep loss) keeping inflammation simmering.
- **MINIMAL (fires, no resolution):** chronic social isolation or status stress defeating the inflammatory off-switch.
- **DEFEATED (fires, no resolution):** self-sustaining chronic low-grade inflammation - allostatic-load collapse.

### What goes wrong in modern life

Chronic low-grade inflammation from diet, sedentary living, and social isolation keeps the system activated with nothing to actually clear.

### How well-evidenced

Forced, 37 evidence links - all five rungs evidenced. The most contested call is whether lifestyle drivers really rank above social isolation. Key researchers: Dantzer, Danese, Irwin. About 600 million years old, present in all multicellular life.

## M9 - Care / Alloparenting

### What it does

This is the parental and alloparental care system - "allo" meaning care from someone other than the parent. It runs on oxytocin and prolactin. The crucial point is that humans evolved to raise children in a village, not alone: the organism expects distributed care.

### What actually satisfies it

A distributed care network rather than solo or two-person parenting. Traditional societies typically show multiple non-parent caregivers (around 4 or more). What resolves the system is the presence and continuity of real alloparental supply - actual care and provisioning - not mere blood relationship.

### The proxy gradient

This mechanism has **two separate scales**, not one ladder. We present them separately.

**Axis A - real alloparental supply** (a deprivation axis, ordered by how many caregivers there are and how continuous they are):

- **FULL (need met):** a distributed network of about 4 or more kin and community members.
- **REDUCED (need met):** multiple reliable caregivers, including high-quality paid care.
- **THIN (partly resolved):** a single intermittent helper, or high-turnover institutional care.
- **EMPTY SHELF (fires, no resolution):** a solo or two-person parent with no network. (This is a deprivation floor, not a proxy.)

**Axis B - the fake-fix proxy** (real alloparental function is essentially zero here):

- **ZERO (fires, no resolution):** a screen or app babysitter, escalating to an AI or robot "caregiver." (Derived from the architecture.)

### What goes wrong in modern life

The nuclear family without an alloparental network leaves parents carrying a load the system never expected - leading to a burnout cascade.

### How well-evidenced

Forced, 64 evidence links. Key researchers: Hrdy, Panksepp, Kenkel. About 200 million years old.

## M10 - Movement / Regulatory

### What it does

Movement is far more than exercise. It acts as a timing cue for the body's clock, an anti-inflammatory, and a mood regulator, switching on repair processes throughout the organism.

### What actually satisfies it

Movement woven into daily life rather than set aside as "exercise." Hunter-gatherers typically moved 9 to 15 kilometers a day simply as a side effect of how they lived.

### The proxy gradient

This gradient inverts the usual proxy story: the real input is withdrawn rather than a false cue fired. It is ordered by how much of the all-day pattern of muscle activity is supplied:

- **FULL (need met):** all-day incidental movement of foraging life - locomotion plus active resting postures.
- **ATTENUATED (need met):** a physically active job, such as farming, manual labor, or a walking-heavy trade.
- **PARTIAL (partly resolved):** a mostly sedentary day broken up by walking, standing, and fidgeting - incidental movement.
- **MINIMAL (fires, no resolution):** a sedentary day plus a discrete gym session - a partial re-supply, not a false cue.
- **DEFEATED (fires, no resolution):** chair-bound sedentarism with essentially no movement.

### What goes wrong in modern life

Sedentary living, with gym sessions as only partial compensation.

### How well-evidenced

This is rated strongly supported (Tier 2) rather than forced. It has 29 evidence links, all five rungs evidenced. The most debatable point is ranking the gym below all-day incidental movement - the order would flip if you scored by fitness rather than by the activity distribution. Key researchers: Pontzer, Lieberman, Ratey. About 500 million years old. Suppressed by threat and grief.

## M11 - Cooperation / Alliance

### What it does

This is the machinery for reciprocal exchange, detecting cheaters, enforcing norms, and forming coalitions - the sense of who is on your team. An honest note on its rank: no single line of evidence forces a dedicated mechanism here, but four converging research streams strongly support it.

### What actually satisfies it

A trusted reciprocal network with norm enforcement, inside a group small enough for direct reputation tracking - about 150 people, the same Dunbar ceiling that bounds social bonding. Enforcing norms requires repeated direct contact where cheaters can be identified, remembered, and sanctioned. Beyond ~150, cooperation needs institutional scaffolding - formal rules, third-party enforcement, shared fictions.

### The proxy gradient

The cue is the constant alliance-and-reciprocity machinery. Importantly, the step from FULL to PARTIAL is resolution-by-prosthesis: institutions genuinely re-supply the function past ~150. Real proxy failure only begins at MIXED:

- **FULL (need met):** a trusted reciprocal network under ~150, with direct reputation tracking and norm enforcement.
- **PARTIAL (partly resolved):** institutionally scaffolded cooperation beyond ~150 - formal rules, third-party enforcement, shared fictions.
- **MIXED (partly resolved):** market exchange among strangers backed by gameable reputation systems.
- **MINIMAL (fires, no resolution):** coalition-signaling and parasocial alliance through symbolic in-group markers.
- **ZERO (fires, no resolution):** anonymous one-shot transactions and AI "allies" with no reputation and no shared narrative. (Derived from the architecture.)

### What goes wrong in modern life

Anonymous transactions, ghosting, and the absence of any shared narrative leave the cooperation machinery firing without real reciprocal partners.

### How well-evidenced

Strongly supported (Tier 2), 73 evidence links. The most contested point is exactly where proxy failure begins, at the PARTIAL/MIXED boundary. Key researchers: Trivers, Boehm, Fehr & Gächter, Tomasello. About 2 to 5 million years old, shared with great apes. Suppressed by reproduction.

## M12 - Contamination Avoidance

### What it does

This is the disgust system - the "behavioral immune system." It spans three domains: pathogen disgust (rotten food), sexual disgust, and moral disgust. Pathogen avoidance appears to have been extended into social and moral judgments.

### What actually satisfies it

Clean food, a clean social environment, and moral disgust contained. (That last clause is flagged as contested.)

### The proxy gradient

The well-evidenced gradient covers pathogen disgust and has three rungs:

- **FULL (need met):** clean food and a clean social environment, with disgust firing on real pathogen cues.
- **PARTIAL (partly resolved):** vulnerability-calibrated over-firing - for example during pregnancy or immunosuppression, when the system understandably gets more sensitive.
- **MIXED (partly resolved):** behavioral-immune false alarms - the smoke-detector reflex firing on harmless people. (This is the floor.)

There is also a **demoted, non-ordinal** extension covering moral disgust: purity-coded social aversion, weaponized moral disgust, and purity spirals. These are **not rungs** on the ladder. Research (Tybur) suggests moral disgust is functionally distinct, sharing only the facial expression with pathogen disgust. This extension rests on the weakest evidence, and the prediction below lives entirely here.

### What goes wrong in modern life

Moral disgust gets weaponized in politics, fueling purity spirals.

### How well-evidenced

Strongly supported (Tier 2), but with only 12 evidence links - the thinnest in the framework. Key researchers: Rozin, Curtis, Schaller. About 300 million years old.

## M13 - Energy Regulation

### What it does

This system governs hunger, satiety (the feeling of fullness), and total energy expenditure. Following Pontzer's constrained-energy model, the key insight is that the body redistributes energy rather than simply burning more of it.

### What actually satisfies it

Effortful, diverse, seasonal whole foods - metabolic variety.

### The proxy gradient

Here the satiety signal itself - the "stop eating" message - is progressively disabled and finally defeated. The scale is food-processing intensity:

- **FULL (need met):** effortful, diverse, seasonal whole foods.
- **ATTENUATED (need met):** minimally processed traditional whole foods.
- **PARTIAL (partly resolved):** conventionally processed, energy-dense food.
- **MINIMAL (fires, no resolution):** energy-dense palatable food optimized to make you crave it.
- **DEFEATED (fires, no resolution):** ultra-processed "bliss-point" food - an engineered supernormal stimulus.

### What goes wrong in modern life

Ultra-processed foods are engineered to defeat the satiety signal, so the "stop" message never lands.

### How well-evidenced

Strongly supported (Tier 2), 34 evidence links. The endpoints are well-evidenced; the middle three rungs are derived from the architecture, and it is debatable whether they are three real tiers or just a whole-versus-ultra-processed split. Key researchers: Pontzer, Keys. About 500 million years old.

## M14 - Reproductive Motivation

### What it does

This system, following Fisher's three-system model, has three components that can run independently: desire, attraction, and pair-bonding. It is distinct from general-purpose pursuit (M2) and runs on the body's hormonal HPG axis, not just brain circuits. It shapes far more of human behavior than reproduction alone.

### What actually satisfies it

Sexual fulfillment within a pair bond, pair-bond stability, and reproductive success.

### The proxy gradient

The cue stays the same. What changes is how much of a real partner is present:

- **FULL (need met):** a real partner.
- **PARTIAL (partly resolved):** an attenuated real relationship, such as a long-distance relationship.
- **MIXED (partly resolved):** a partial proxy - a dating app that leads to real meetings.
- **MINIMAL (fires, no resolution):** a soft pure proxy - romance fiction or parasocial attachment.
- **ZERO (fires, no resolution):** a hard pure proxy - pornography or AI sexual products.

### What goes wrong in modern life

Pornography, dating apps, delayed reproduction, and social-media mate-value comparison all fire the system without supplying a stable bond.

### How well-evidenced

Forced, 58 evidence links. Key researchers: Panksepp, Fisher, Buss, Trivers, Hrdy. The sexual component is about 500 million years old; pair-bonding about 200 million. This is one of the systems with a **sex-differentiated implementation** - the same system, two settings. The female-typical version is VMH-centered, tied to estrous cycling, with a preference for resource and commitment signals and an emphasis on pair-bond security. The male-typical version is POA-centered, driven by tonic testosterone, attentive to fertility and accessibility cues, with an emphasis on sexual variety. It is suppressed by threat, immune activation, and care; it suppresses agency and cooperation.

## R1 - Touch

### What it does

Touch is not a motivational system in its own right. It is a regulatory input that feeds the social bonding system (M3), carried by specialized nerves called C-tactile afferents. Think of it as a channel that helps tune bonding, rather than a drive that pushes you.

### What actually satisfies it

Regular physical contact with trusted others.

### The proxy gradient

This gradient is flagged as contested - the very premise that the cue and its function can be cleanly separated is disputed here. The rungs are ordered by how much real relational function remains:

- **FULL (need met):** affectionate touch from a trusted, reciprocating other.
- **PARTIAL (partly resolved):** affective touch from a non-intimate caregiver, such as massage.
- **MIXED (partly resolved):** incidental, non-affectionate everyday contact.
- **MINIMAL (fires, no resolution):** a weighted or deep-pressure substitute, with no living other present. (Speculative.)
- **ZERO (fires, no resolution):** synthetic, haptic, or AI C-tactile stimulation with zero relational embedding. (Speculative.)

Note: the bottom two rungs both carry zero relational function and are separated only by a secondary, cue-magnitude axis.

### What goes wrong in modern life

We live in touch-starved environments. One striking piece of evidence: kangaroo care - skin-to-skin contact - is associated with a 40% reduction in mortality in premature infants.

### How well-evidenced

This is the most tentative entry, rated moderate (Tier 3), with 18 evidence links. Key researchers: McGlone, Field. About 200 million years old, mammalian.

## The Gap - the matched environment vs the modern default

Here is the same idea as a list you can scan: for each part of life, what the world our biology expects used to provide, versus what it provides now. The number in parentheses is how well-evidenced each one is - many rest on a single small study, so treat the figures as illustrative, not exact.

> This section holds EEA numbers NEXT TO the spec, not inside it: each parameter is a mechanism's resolution baseline + the modern default + the gap between them. Grades are inherited from the evidence and reuse Cor's own vocabulary. 'replicated' appears only twice. Mismatch interpretations are capped at 'theoretical' (the honesty floor); the original-affluent-society work-hours debate is 'contested' with no number asserted; most of information_environment is held out. Exact figures from single forager populations are ILLUSTRATIVE, NOT CONSTANTS.

**32 parameters across 8 domains** (grades: theoretical 16, thin 5, contested 4, clinical 3, replicated 2, longitudinal 2; 8 carry a contested flag).
### Social structure

**Social network layers (~5/15/50/150)** (grade: replicated)
  - The mismatch is not in the inner-layer capacities themselves (still ~5/15/50/150, not technologically expandable) but between the number of ties the modern environment presents (hundreds to thousands of nominal contacts) and the number the brain can actually monitor and invest in (~150, with real support concentrated in the inner ~5-15). People with unusually large networks do not gain more close friends; they accumulate weakly-defined acquaintances. Status monitoring (M5) and alliance/reputation tracking (M11), evolved for a ~150 personally-known reference group, are now flooded by reference groups orders of magnitude larger and largely composed of strangers, while the trust-bearing inner layers remain capped and are under-fed when face-to-face contact is displaced.

**Lifetime known-individual ceiling (~1,000)** (grade: theoretical · CONTESTED)
  - EEA delivered a finite, slowly-accreting lifetime roster (~1,000+) in which virtually every encountered person was known or one reciprocal link away and carried trackable reputation; modernity delivers an unbounded firehose of identities, the overwhelming majority anonymous, non-reciprocal, and reputation-less. The mismatch is not in the size of the maintainable network (still ~150) but in the ratio of known-to-unknown: a system tuned to "almost everyone you meet is someone you can place" now operates where almost everyone you meet is someone you cannot. This chronically engages strangers as ambient novelty/threat (threat-management framing) and starves the cooperation/reputation system (M11) of the direct, repeated, reciprocal observability it evolved to run on, while the cumulative roster of genuinely-known people fails to grow despite massive nominal connection.

**Band / camp size (a few dozen adults; ~25-50)** (grade: thin · CONTESTED)
  - The ancestral social field was a small set of a few dozen (~25-50) personally-known adults sharing a camp, sitting within the band/overnight-camp Dunbar layer. The modern default replaces this with chronic immersion in groupings far larger than the band scale, dominated by unfamiliar people, while genuinely co-regulating relationships number far fewer than the band once held. The delta is a mismatch between the scale at which our cooperation, status-monitoring, and bonding machinery was calibrated (a bounded, fully-known band/camp of a few dozen adults) and the scale we actually inhabit (large, partly anonymous aggregations plus a thin set of disposable ties), so the band-sized layer that once carried daily social life is no longer delivered by any single modern structure.

**Within-camp kin fraction (low; campmates mostly unrelated)** (grade: thin)
  - The ancestral signal was: 'the group around me is mostly unrelated people I (or my band) actively chose and continuously vetted through reciprocity and reputation; kinship is not what holds us together, the relationships are.' The modern signal is degraded on the selection axis: the unrelated people around us are still mostly unrelated (that part matches), but they are assigned rather than chosen, and the reciprocity/reputation machinery that turned unrelated campmates into durable trusted allies is largely absent or shallow. Mechanisms M3 (bonding) and M11 (cooperation/alliance) evolved expecting that low kin-relatedness would be compensated by self-selected, reciprocal, reputation-anchored dyads. Modernity delivers the low relatedness WITHOUT the compensating chosen-and-vetted dyadic structure, so the mechanisms fire in a world of unrelated-but-also-unbonded co-residents, which is a key part of why being surrounded by people can still register as social isolation.

**Membership turnover (camp dissolves and reconstitutes; ~12% repeated campmates year-over-year, ~88% annual turnover)** (grade: thin)
  - The ancestral and modern environments are inverted on which layer is stable. Ancestrally the GROUP churned (~88% of campmates turned over yearly) while the durable reciprocal DYAD was conserved across the churn; the relationship, not the group, was the unit that persisted. Modernity delivers the opposite: stable, durable group labels (workplace, cohort, feed) paired with shallow, disposable dyads and no mechanism (no hxaro-equivalent) for conserving a specific bond once proximity ends. The mismatch is not 'too much turnover' versus 'too little' - it is that turnover now lands on the relationships (which evolved to persist) instead of on group membership (which evolved to be fluid), leaving the co-regulating, reputation-tracking reciprocal dyad chronically unmet.

**Selection rule / "shuffle algorithm" (choice-driven; homophily + cooperation + reciprocity; kinship not main glue)** (grade: thin)
  - The EEA delivered a continuous, agency-rich sorting process that filtered one's immediate social world toward reciprocating, cooperative, vouched-for partners - kinship was secondary. The modern environment inverts this: membership in one's daily groups is mostly imposed (employer, building, algorithm), and the explicit matching systems that do exist optimize for engagement/novelty rather than reciprocity and demonstrated cooperation. The result is reduced control over WHO populates one's social field, weaker assortment on actual cooperativeness, and loss of the transitive-reputation signal that let ancestral humans cluster with trustworthy reciprocators out to two degrees. The mismatch is not "fewer people" but a broken selection rule: the inputs the ancestral algorithm ran on (mutual choice, observed reciprocity, cooperator reputation) are no longer the primary determinants of who one lives and works among.

**Relationship longevity & maintenance (decades-long durable dyads; ~27-yr co-survival ceiling; maintenance institutions like hxaro conserve bonds across distance)** (grade: thin)
  - The ancestral environment conserved the relationship across changing groups; the modern environment conserves the group label and lets the relationships churn. The specific missing piece is a maintenance institution: the EEA had hxaro-type structures (formalized, inheritable, distance-spanning, lifelong delayed reciprocity) that actively preserved a named dyad even with little or no co-presence, whereas modernity has no hxaro-equivalent and instead relies on shared proximity/context as the only glue. When that proximity ends (job change, move, leaving school), the bond is not migrated to an institution that keeps it alive; it decays. The result is many stable-looking memberships sitting on top of disposable, shallow ties, with the durable decades-long reciprocal partnership that M3/M11 are calibrated to expect largely absent.

**Novel-stranger frequency (rare, salient, often a threat)** (grade: theoretical)
  - The delta is one of base rate and resolvability. Ancestrally, novel strangers were rare, salient, and resolvable (place them in the known web, or treat unfamiliarity as an actionable threat signal); modernity inverts this to constant, non-diagnostic, unresolvable novelty. The threat-management system inherited from the rare-stranger regime cannot down-regulate to the new base rate: it either over-fires (chronic low-grade vigilance, stranger-directed wariness in crowds and online) or, where it habituates, leaves people transacting with a stream of others who carry none of the reputational accountability the system implicitly assumes. The conserved unit being eroded is not the count of strangers but the durable, reputationally-tracked dyad against which any new face used to be evaluated; once almost everyone is a stranger, the bounded-pool premise that made novelty meaningful no longer holds.

**Behavioral observability / reputation visibility** (grade: theoretical)
  - The ancestral environment delivered near-total, embodied, hard-to-fake, single-identity behavioral observability inside a ~150-person reference group whose members knew one's history and held real, inescapable enforcement power. The modern environment delivers visibility that is mediated, curated, gameable, fragmented across exitable contexts, padded with non-accountable weak ties, and largely stripped of peer-enforced consequences. The reputation-tracking machinery (M11) and status-position monitoring (M5) evolved to run on dense, durable, accountable observation now operate on thin, performative, low-accountability signals, producing both reputational impunity for defection among strangers and chronic exposure of curated personas to vast unfamiliar audiences with no reciprocal knowledge of the person behind them.


### Care & development

**Alloparental care supply (a village, not a dyad)** (grade: theoretical)
  - The mismatch is between the reliable, present, multi-caregiver surround the infant's attachment and the mother's reproductive psychology evolved to expect, and the thin one- or two-adult household the modern environment supplies. The load that was carried by a village now falls on isolated parents; maternal investment is calibrated to assess available allomaternal support (M9), so its evolved absence produces overload, depleted caregivers, and infants with fewer attachment figures than the cooperative-breeding design assumes. This is a supply mismatch, not a parental-competence failure.

**Caregiver continuity and reliability (present, not merely countable)** (grade: theoretical)
  - The mismatch is between caregiver reliability/continuity as an experienced, present regulatory input and a modern environment that often supplies caregivers who are countable but not dependably present. Because maternal investment and infant attachment are both calibrated to perceived caregiver reliability (M9, R1), low continuity is not neutral: it degrades the secure-base scaffolding and the touch-mediated regulation the infant evolved to expect, even when the number of caregivers on paper looks adequate.

**Mixed-age child playgroups as developmental input** (grade: theoretical)
  - The mismatch is between mixed-age, self-organized, physically-active play as the evolved calibrating input for the juvenile prefrontal cortex (M4, embedded in the alloparental child-minding network, M9) and a modern environment that supplies age-segregated, adult-structured, often sedentary substitutes. Because play partners causally shape PFC development and social-reciprocity circuits, reduced free play is plausibly a genuine developmental-input deficit, not merely a lifestyle change.

**Multigenerational presence (grandmothers as provisioning kin)** (grade: theoretical)
  - The mismatch is between the present, active, provisioning grandparental generation the cooperative-breeding system expects as part of allomaternal supply (M9, M3) and the modern default of geographically separated or non-provisioning grandparents. The grandmother's evolved contribution to inclusive fitness and to the caloric and knowledge subsidy of grandchildren is structurally absent for many modern families, thinning the alloparental network exactly where it was most reliably staffed.


### Touch & contact

**Affective (C-tactile) contact frequency and skin-to-skin time** (grade: clinical)
  - Modern environments routinely fall below the ancestral affective-touch floor. Touch deprivation in extreme cases (institutional rearing) produces growth-hormone suppression, immune dysfunction, and developmental arrest; even in non-extreme modern life the loss of moderate-pressure affective touch removes a direct oxytocin/vagal/cortisol-regulating input. Critically, affective touch is the one ancestral channel that remote, volumetric, or screen-based technology cannot deliver: it requires physical skin contact.

**Co-sleeping / nighttime body contact** (grade: contested · CONTESTED)
  - Solitary infant and child sleep removes the nighttime half of the ancestral continuous-contact regime, eliminating touch-based physiological co-regulation during sleep. This is a plausible but, in the present corpus, evidentially thin extension of the daytime affective-touch and attachment findings; it should not be over-stated.

**Affective grooming/touch from trusted others (adult social touch)** (grade: clinical)
  - Loss of routine affiliative touch from trusted others removes a direct endorphin-bonding and cortisol/cardiovascular stress-buffering input that operated continuously in ancestral social life. Partner/trusted-other touch buffers acute stress more effectively than verbal support alone, and the benefit is bidirectional. This channel is physical-contact-only and cannot be delivered by remote or volumetric technology.


### Circadian & sensory

**Dawn/dusk light gradient with genuine nocturnal darkness as circadian entrainment signal** (grade: theoretical)
  - The modern light environment inverts the ancestral pattern on both ends: too little bright light by day, too much blue-enriched light at night, with no real darkness. This delays circadian phase, compresses and fragments the biological night, and decouples internal time from external solar time.

**Sleep timing/phase alignment to the biological night (not a fixed duration target)** (grade: longitudinal)
  - The mismatch is primarily one of phase, not amount. Even when total sleep is adequate, misalignment between biological and social clocks is associated with metabolic harm (e.g. social jetlag raises overweight odds and predicts BMI in the overweight, with an effect more than half that of sleep duration, after controlling for duration itself). Chasing a duration number while ignoring phase misses the operative variable.

**Falling evening ambient temperature as a co-primary sleep-onset cue** (grade: theoretical)
  - By flattening the daily thermal cycle, modern housing removes a candidate onset cue that historically co-occurred with dusk and reinforced circadian timing, potentially leaving light as the sole (and now corrupted) entrainment channel.


### Subsistence & time-use

**Daily incidental movement load** (grade: theoretical)
  - The gap is not a calorie or PAL gap (these are surprisingly close) but a distributional one: ancestral movement was low-intensity, continuous, and obligatory; modern movement is optional, bunched, and easily skipped, leaving long uninterrupted muscular-inactivity bouts that the body never encountered. The mismatch is in HOW the day's muscle activity is spread, not in gross energy turnover.

**Effort-to-outcome contingency (closed-loop, self-attributable)** (grade: longitudinal)
  - Because passivity is the unlearned default that only switches OFF when controllability is detected, environments that obscure the effort-outcome link don't feel neutral - they default the organism toward helplessness-type physiology. The gap is the loss of routinely detectable, self-attributable contingency, which population health data link to elevated disease risk (low job control predicts coronary disease independent of grade).

**Task diversity within the subsistence day** (grade: theoretical)
  - The gap is the collapse from a high-variety, skill-diverse subsistence portfolio to narrow repetitive specialization, under-engaging the exploratory-pursuit system whose canonical evolved function is varied engagement with a changing world. This row is included because it meets the 3-part rule, but the EEA quantification of 'task diversity' is not pinned down in Cor's evidence.

**Daily subsistence work-hours (contested)** (grade: contested · CONTESTED)
  - The defensible mismatch is in the STRUCTURE of work-time (externally clocked, continuous, boundary-less vs. intermittent, self-paced, contingent), not in any claimed reduction or increase in daily hours. Asserting a specific ancestral work-hour number - in either direction - would import a contested anthropological position as if it were settled, which it is not.


### Diet & metabolic

**Food diversity and seasonality (whole-food, micronutrient-dense, omnivorous variety)** (grade: theoretical · CONTESTED)
  - The modern food environment collapses a seasonally varied, micronutrient-dense, whole-food omnivory into a year-round monotony of refined, energy-dense, nutrient-diluted products, removing both the variety and the whole-food matrix the metabolic and digestive systems were calibrated to.

**Satiety-honest fiber and whole-food matrix (food that signals fullness truthfully)** (grade: theoretical · CONTESTED)
  - Industrial formulation deliberately separates the reward cues (sweet/fat/salt) from the satiety cues (fiber, bulk, slow digestion) the two were ancestrally bundled together, so modern hyperpalatable food drives consumption past the point the body would otherwise stop, defeating the honest-signal logic of appetite regulation.

**Acquisition effort (calories are physically worked for)** (grade: clinical)
  - Removing the work between hunger and food eliminates a natural brake on consumption and decouples eating from the obligate daily movement it was bundled with - calories are now frictionless to obtain and detached from the physical activity that ancestrally accompanied acquisition.

**Energy regulation is intake-bounded (total expenditure is homeostatically buffered, not behavior-throttled)** (grade: replicated)
  - Modern environments leave intake unbounded (cheap, hyperpalatable, effortless calories) while the body's expenditure stays homeostatically capped - so the one variable the system relied on as the binding constraint (limited, worked-for food) is exactly the one industrialization removed, and the popular 'just exercise it off' remedy targets the wrong, buffered side.


### Threat & safety

**Threat temporality (acute, episodic, escapable-by-action)** (grade: theoretical)
  - The mismatch is in the time-course, not the threat machinery itself. The system is calibrated for brief, resolvable spikes; modern life supplies sustained, unresolved activation. Acute stress is adaptive (even enhances immunity); the identical response sustained chronically suppresses immunity and accumulates wear-and-tear. The gap is the absence of an episode boundary that lets the response shut down.

**Threat controllability and predictability (resolvable by own action)** (grade: theoretical)
  - What is pathogenic is not threat per se but uncontrollability/unpredictability: the absence of an action that resolves the danger. Lab work shows lack of predictability and control is so aversive that subjects choose a stronger immediate shock over waiting, and chronic uncontrollable stress reproduces eight of nine DSM depression symptoms. Modern environments systematically supply unresolvable, action-proof stressors - the mismatch.

**Real-vs-perceived threat ratio and all-clear resolution** (grade: theoretical)
  - The pathogenic feature is the broken resolution loop: ancestrally, alarms (real or false) closed with an all-clear; modern abstract threats supply alarms that never close. Chronically unbalanced body budgeting and unbridled inflammation are implicated in chronic stress, anxiety, depression and physical disease (Barrett; McEwen allostatic load). The mismatch is the missing terminating action and missing social all-clear, not the false-alarm rate itself - false alarms were always common and were tolerated precisely because they resolved.


### Information environment

**Signal provenance and accountability (information from known, reputation-bearing, face-to-face sources)** (grade: contested · CONTESTED)
  - The evolved machinery for weighing information by source reputation and face-to-face accountability is deprived of the provenance cues it was calibrated to use. Receivers must evaluate high-stakes claims from sources they cannot place in any reputational web, and senders bear little reputational cost for deception - removing the gossip/ostracism feedback loop that ancestrally policed signal honesty.

**Gossip and narrative flow as reputation and norm transmission** (grade: contested · CONTESTED)
  - The gossip/narrative channel that ancestrally fused information transmission with reputation tracking and norm enforcement is decoupled. People consume reputational narrative about strangers they cannot sanction, and the norm-deterrence function (knowing the group is watching and will talk) is weakened in anonymous or one-to-many settings - leaving the evolved appetite for reputational narrative engaged but its enforcement payload disabled.

## About this framework

Modern human suffering is accurate biological signaling in a mismatched environment, not disorder in the individual. Preferences are mechanism outputs, not ground truth. The load-bearing claim of every proxy gradient: the cue is identical at every level; only the resolving function changes, so a proxy can fire the cue without supplying what actually resolves it.
