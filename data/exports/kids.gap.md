## The Gap - then and now

A long time ago, people lived in small groups, played and moved around all day, saw the sun come up and the stars at night, and stayed close to the people they loved.

Our bodies still expect that world. But today a lot of it has changed - bright screens at night, sitting still a lot, snacks that are made to taste amazing, and lots of strangers instead of a few close friends.

That's "the gap": the space between what our bodies expect and what they get now. It's why some things feel hard even when nothing is wrong with you. (These are general patterns scientists worked out from studying people - real ideas, but not exact rules.)

The tricky part: a pretend version can trick your brain into feeling like it counts - like a game that gives you the *feeling* of friends without real friends. Knowing about the gap helps you spot the difference.
