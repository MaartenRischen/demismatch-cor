## How to read this document

This document is a working map of human motivation and distress, written for people who sit across from other people and try to help them. Its central claim will reframe a lot of what you see in your office: most of the suffering your clients bring you is not a defect in them. It is accurate biological signaling coming from systems that are working correctly, but being fed the wrong inputs by a modern environment they were never built for.

Put plainly: the feeling is real, the signal is informative, and the problem is usually upstream of the person. When someone feels anxious, flat, restless, ashamed, or empty, the default reading here is not "this person is broken" but "this system is reporting on its input conditions." That is a diagnostic stance, not a romantic one. It does not mean the signal is always literally true, and it does not mean you should withhold symptom relief where it is indicated. It does mean you check the inputs before you conclude the machinery is faulty.

There are well-defined exceptions where the signal stops being a reliable readout: developmental miscalibration carried forward from early life, chronic dysregulation, acute organic disease or state shifts (sleep deprivation, inflammation, endocrine disruption, delirium), structural damage, and substance or medication effects. In those cases the signal can be decoupled from current conditions, and you treat accordingly.

### The derivation stack, briefly

The framework is built in layers, each forced by the one beneath it:

- **Frame:** We never get unmediated access to reality. We get evolved interfaces that were selected to keep our ancestors alive and reproducing, not to tell us the truth. And the default reading of a motivational or emotional output is that it is information about conditions, not noise to suppress.
- **Premise:** The loss function evolution optimized is inclusive fitness - survival and reproduction. The organism is not a general-purpose learner; it is a bundle of specialized, interacting systems. Modern environments routinely push those systems outside the ranges they expect.
- **Properties:** From this it follows that many painful outputs are intelligible defensive signals, that human regulation is built to run on other people, that systems are coupled and can drag each other down, that proxy cues can switch a system on without ever switching it off, that defensive systems are biased toward false alarms, and that fixing one system does not generically fix another.
- **Consequences:** Chronic unresolved activation accumulates as bodily wear (allostatic load). Markets learn to sell proxies for unmet needs. And because cortical override is metabolically expensive and degrades under chronic load, changing the actual inputs is usually more durable than asking the client to think their way out. The environment is a primary intervention layer - and it is the one most institutions underweight.

This last point is the clinical heart of the document. Where indicated, individual cognitive, pharmacological, and psychotherapeutic work belongs in the plan. But the default leverage is in changing inputs, not in patching the person.

### How to read a proxy gradient

This is the single most useful tool here, so read it carefully.

Each system fires in response to a **cue**. The cue is identical at every level of the gradient. What changes as you go down the ladder is the **resolving function** - whether anything that actually satisfies and terminates the system is being supplied.

A proxy is something that fires the cue without supplying what resolves it. At the top of a gradient (FULL), the cue fires and the need is met. As you descend, the cue keeps firing just as strongly, but less and less real resolution arrives, until at the floor the cue fires and nothing is supplied at all. That is the open loop: the system is switched on, the client feels the pull, and it never switches off.

For each mechanism below you will get: what it looks like in a person, the full proxy gradient in applied terms, the mismatch prediction (what specifically goes wrong when a modern environment feeds it proxies), how well-grounded the claim is (evidence-link count and key researchers), and what changing the inputs - rather than patching the person - would mean.

A note on the ladders: most are simple top-to-bottom ordinal ladders. A few systems behave differently. Some (marked Kind B) are not about a false cue being fired but about a real input being withdrawn or a regulatory off-switch being disabled. Where I use level labels (FULL, PARTIAL, MIXED, MINIMAL, ZERO, and the like), I am using only the examples the framework itself supplies.

## M1 - Threat Management

### What it looks like in a person

This is the freeze-fight-flee system, plus the offensive mode of frustration-aggression. In the room, it shows up as panic, hypervigilance, a racing heart, irritability, a client who is "always on," who cannot relax, who startles, who is exhausted by a danger they cannot name. It is mediated by phylogenetically ancient subcortical circuits - amygdala, periaqueductal gray, hypothalamus - that operate pre-attentively and can override cortical processing. This is roughly 500 million years old, shared by all vertebrates, and it is a single shared implementation across the sexes.

What resolves it is simple and physical: safety established, the threat eliminated or avoided.

### The proxy gradient (resolvability axis)

- **FULL:** acute danger you can fight or flee, then escape. The system fires, you act, you get away, it shuts off. Clean resolution.
- **PARTIAL:** a real present threat resolved by a trusted other. Partly resolved - someone helps you handle it.
- **MIXED:** a chronic slow-burn real threat with no terminating action available. The danger is real but there is nothing to fight and nowhere to flee.
- **MINIMAL:** an abstract or anticipated threat with no physical target. The cue fires, but there is nothing to act on, so nothing resolves.
- **ZERO:** engineered supernormal outrage and threat media. The cue is manufactured and fired at you; there is nothing real to resolve at all. (This floor rung is flagged contestable.)

The cue feels identical at every rung - the same dread, the same activation. Only the availability of resolving action drains away as you descend.

### Mismatch prediction

Modern threats - debt, emails, social media, status anxiety - cannot be fought or fled. The system stays on. This is the core problem: M1 was built for threats that end. Modern threats do not end, so the system never gets the "safe now" signal it is built to wait for.

### Cascade

M1 is powerful. It suppresses pursuit (M2), play (M4), reproductive motivation (M14), movement (M10), and status monitoring (M6 - controllability). When threat is high, exploration, play, sex, exercise, and a sense of agency all go offline - which is exactly what you see in a chronically threatened client. M1 is itself suppressed only by M3 (social bonding): a trusted other present can turn the alarm down.

### What changing the inputs means

Patching the person means teaching them to override an ancient alarm with expensive cortical effort - which degrades under chronic load. Changing the inputs means asking what unresolvable threat the system is correctly reporting (financial, occupational, relational, informational) and either making it resolvable or removing it. The PARTIAL rung is a clinical lever in itself: a trusted other can resolve a real threat the person cannot resolve alone.

### Evidence

115 evidence links. Key researchers: Panksepp, LeDoux, Mobbs. This mechanism is **forced** by convergence C10.

## M2 - Pursuit / Exploration

### What it looks like in a person

This is the general-purpose SEEKING system - the drive to explore, pursue goals, build skills, and engage with the world. Note the scope change: sexual motivation has been moved out of M2 into M14. What is left is narrow and specific. In a person it looks like drive and curiosity when healthy, and like restless, compulsive, never-satisfied "wanting" when hijacked - the client who scrolls for hours, chases novelty, feels driven but never full.

The key dissociation: wanting (mesolimbic dopamine) is separable from liking (hedonic satisfaction). A proxy can crank up the wanting while delivering none of the liking that normally closes the loop.

What resolves it: engagement with real challenges that produce tangible outcomes.

### The proxy gradient (cue = SEEKING/wanting, constant throughout)

- **FULL:** engaging a real challenge that yields a real outcome. (Evidenced.)
- **PARTIAL:** knowledge work with a real but abstracted, delayed outcome.
- **MIXED:** a variable-reward app or feed with intermittent real payoff.
- **MINIMAL:** infinite novelty, content scroll, idle browsing - cue fires, nothing resolves.
- **ZERO:** machine gambling engineered for time-on-device with guaranteed net loss. (Evidenced.) Cue fires; not only does nothing resolve, the player is systematically drained.

The wanting is identical at the top and the bottom. What drains as you descend is the tangible outcome and the consummatory closure that would say "enough." (The framework notes the MIXED-vs-MINIMAL ordering is its most contestable point.)

### Mismatch prediction

Variable-reward apps fire SEEKING without resolution. Open loop. This is the textbook case: the cue is maximal, the resolving function is zero, and the system runs all day.

### Cascade

M2 is suppressed by threat (M1), bonding (M3), and several Tier-2 systems (M7 circadian, M8 immune, M13 energy). It interacts with M4 (play) and M14 (reproduction). Practically: a client whose SEEKING looks dead may be downstream of poor sleep, inflammation, threat, or grief - check those before concluding the drive itself is the problem.

### What changing the inputs means

You cannot satisfy a hijacked SEEKING system with more of the proxy - that is the open loop. You resolve it by supplying real challenges with tangible outcomes (the FULL rung) and reducing exposure to the engineered floor rungs. Patching the person with willpower against an infinite feed is the losing strategy.

### Evidence

67 evidence links. Key researchers: Panksepp, Berridge. This mechanism is **forced** by convergence C6.

## M3 - Social Bonding

### What it looks like in a person

This is attachment and co-regulation, and it now also includes grief (absorbed from the old GRIEF system). It runs in two states: security, which enables the person broadly, and grief, which suppresses broadly. In the room: the securely-bonded client recovers from stress faster and explores more; the grieving or isolated client looks shut down across the board. This is roughly 200 million years old (mammalian), with 192 evidence links - the most heavily grounded mechanism in the entire atlas.

What resolves it is reliable human co-regulation through physical presence, organized in nested layers: about 5 intimate co-regulators who provide most of the regulation, about 15 in the sympathy group, about 50 close partners, and about 150 coherent face-to-face relationships (the Dunbar number, the outer cognitive ceiling). The critical variable at the innermost layer of about 5 is **reliability, not count**.

### The proxy gradient (ordered by real co-regulatory function, not amount of contact)

- **FULL:** a reliable, physically co-present inner circle.
- **PARTIAL:** a real bonded inner circle held at a distance via remote contact - real bond, attenuated presence.
- **MIXED:** a large mediated network that routes only partly to real co-presence.
- **MINIMAL:** one-sided parasocial attachment to a non-reciprocating figure - cue fires, no reciprocal regulation.
- **ZERO:** an AI companion engineered to simulate a reciprocating bond. (Evidenced-theoretical.) Cue fires; nothing co-regulates.

Note the off-gradient case: isolated or solo living is not a rung on this ladder - it is empty-shelf deprivation, the absence of input entirely.

### Mismatch prediction

The nuclear family, isolated living, and mediated contact substituting for physical presence. The system expects a reliable physically-present inner circle and a face-to-face network; modern arrangements thin both.

### Cascade

M3 in its security state is the great enabler - it suppresses threat (M1), and it gates pursuit, play, movement, and agency. In its grief state it suppresses them. It is not suppressed by anything in the recorded cascade and interacts with M14. Recall the survival data behind this: strong social relationships confer roughly a 50% survival advantage (Holt-Lunstad).

### What changing the inputs means

This is the highest-leverage cross-cutting input dimension in the framework, because social context scaffolds regulation of nearly everything else. Patching the person means teaching emotion-regulation skills to someone whose architecture expects those skills to be supplied by other bodies in the room. Changing the inputs means building or restoring a reliable physically-present inner circle - prioritizing reliability over count at the innermost layer.

### Evidence

192 evidence links. Key researchers: Bowlby, Panksepp, Coan, Hrdy. This mechanism is **forced** by convergence C5.

## M4 - Social Calibration / Play

### What it looks like in a person

The play system develops social skills through rough-and-tumble. Tellingly, it is absent in threatened or ill animals - so a client (or child) who does not play is telling you something about their threat and health state. In people, especially in development, this shows up as the capacity to read social rules, take turns, recover from minor conflict, and self-direct in a group.

What resolves it: age-matched, self-directed, unsupervised, physical play.

### The proxy gradient (cue = the PLAY/SEEKING social-joy pull; ordered by surviving embodied reciprocity with a real partner)

- **FULL:** age-matched, self-directed, unsupervised physical play with peers. (Evidenced.)
- **PARTIAL:** adult-structured supervised physical activity, organized sports - real bodies, but adult-directed.
- **MIXED:** screen-mediated multiplayer play with real peers - real partners, mediated bodies.
- **MINIMAL:** solitary single-player screen play, no reciprocating partner - cue fires, no real reciprocity.
- **ZERO:** an engineered companion-AI or supernormal variable-reward "play" product. (Architecture-derived.) Cue fires; no identity-bearing partner at all.

(The framework flags the MIXED-over-MINIMAL ranking - screen play with real peers above solitary scripted games - as its most contestable point.)

### Mismatch prediction

Structured activities replacing free play. Screens replacing bodies. Play deprivation. The system needs self-directed embodied reciprocity, and modern childhood substitutes adult structure and screens.

### Cascade

M4 is suppressed by threat (M1) and bonding's grief state (M3) - consistent with its absence in threatened animals - and it interacts with pursuit (M2). This is roughly 200 million years old, present in mammals and absent in reptiles.

### What changing the inputs means

Patching means trying to teach social skills didactically to someone the system expected to learn them through unsupervised peer play. Changing the inputs means restoring access to age-matched, self-directed, unsupervised physical play - and, given the cascade, reducing the threat load that switches play off in the first place.

### Evidence

55 evidence links. Key researchers: Panksepp, Pellis & Pellis, Gray. This mechanism is **forced** by convergences C2, C4, and C12.

## M5 - Status Monitoring

### What it looks like in a person

Rank regulation: shame, pride, submission, defeat, and continuous rank vigilance. This is not optional and it does not turn off - it runs continuously in the background. In the room it presents as chronic comparison, shame spirals, defeat states, and the felt sense of always coming up short. Importantly, the framework distinguishes **earned prestige** (preferred) from **coerced dominance** (Henrich & Gil-White).

What resolves it: an acceptable position within a reference group of about 150, ideally through earned prestige rather than coerced dominance.

### The proxy gradient (cue = constant comparison/ranking signal; ordered by real standing supplied in a real, knowable group)

- **FULL:** earned prestige in a small face-to-face group. (Evidenced.)
- **PARTIAL:** a coerced or unstable dominance rank in a real group. (Evidenced.) Real standing, lower quality.
- **MIXED:** status-striving in a large impersonal society. (Evidenced.)
- **MINIMAL:** global mediated comparison by follower and like counts. (Architecture-derived.) Cue fires, no real resolvable standing.
- **ZERO:** purchased status signals, luxury-brand display. (Architecture-derived.) Cue fires; nothing real is earned.

(The floor ordering - purchased signals vs social-media comparison - is flagged as the most contestable point.)

### Mismatch prediction

Global comparison via social media. Millions of "peers." Status-defeat cascade. The system was built to track standing in a group of about 150; it now reads itself against millions, and a person can lose every comparison.

### Cascade

No recorded suppression in or out; it interacts with M14 (reproduction). What matters clinically is the link to physiology: this mechanism is grounded partly in allostatic-load and primate-stress work (Sapolsky's link between hierarchy type and cortisol), so chronic status defeat is a bodily-wear pathway, not just a mood.

### What changing the inputs means

Patching means trying to talk a client out of comparisons that a continuously-running, non-optional system keeps generating. Changing the inputs means relocating their status competition into a real, knowable, small group where earned prestige is achievable - and reducing exposure to global mediated comparison that the system cannot win.

### Evidence

88 evidence links. Key researchers: Gilbert, Price, Mazur, Sapolsky, Marmot. This mechanism is **forced** by convergences C2, C7, C9, and C13.

## M6 - Controllability / Agency

### What it looks like in a person

This is the vmPFC-DRN controllability circuit. The crucial clinical fact: **learned helplessness is the default; agency is the override.** Passivity is the baseline state the brain falls back to; the sense that your actions matter has to be actively established and maintained. This makes M6 critical for breaking cascades - it is often the system you work through to get the others moving. In a person it looks like passivity, helplessness, "why bother," or, healthily, a felt sense that effort registers.

What resolves it: genuine action-outcome feedback and real decision latitude.

### The proxy gradient (cue = detection of response-outcome contingency)

- **FULL:** decision latitude over consequential outcomes that visibly feed back. (Evidenced.)
- **PARTIAL:** high demand with reduced but still real decision latitude. (Evidenced.)
- **MIXED:** abstract, surveilled, or screen-mediated work where effort does not visibly register. (Evidenced.)
- **MINIMAL:** a chronically uncontrollable real environment producing learned helplessness. (Evidenced.) The cue fires, no real contingency is supplied.
- **ZERO:** an engineered simulated-agency loop - a slot machine, game, or dashboard - firing the contingency cue with zero real consequence. (Architecture-derived.)

This is a dual-axis gradient: from PARTIAL down to MINIMAL, real contingency is progressively withdrawn; ZERO substitutes a false one. (The MINIMAL-vs-ZERO ordering is the contestable point.)

### Mismatch prediction

Abstract work where effort does not visibly register. Rigid hierarchies. Bureaucratic systems. The system needs to see that what it does changes outcomes; modern work often hides that feedback.

### Cascade

M6 is suppressed by threat (M1), reproduction (M14), and bonding's grief state (M3). Because helplessness is the default and agency the override, and because chronic defensive activation degrades the cortical override machinery, this system is fragile under load - and restoring it is often the wedge that lets other cascades reverse.

### What changing the inputs means

Patching means cognitively reframing helplessness in a client whose actual environment supplies no contingency - asking them to feel agentic in a world where their effort genuinely does not register. Changing the inputs means restoring real decision latitude and visible action-outcome feedback. Beware the ZERO rung: simulated-agency loops (games, gamified dashboards) can fire the contingency cue and feel satisfying while supplying zero real consequence.

### Evidence

38 evidence links. Key researchers: Maier & Seligman. This mechanism is **forced** by convergences C8 and C13.

## M7 - Circadian Regulation

### What it looks like in a person

The SCN master clock gates metabolism, immunity, cognition, and mood. This is the oldest system in the atlas - roughly 3 billion years, from cyanobacteria onward. The key clinical insight: the modern mismatch is usually about **timing and quality, not duration**. A client may be "getting eight hours" and still be circadian-disrupted. Presentations include mood disturbance, cognitive fog, metabolic and immune problems - all downstream of a mis-set clock.

What resolves it: dark-cued sleep onset, bright daylight exposure, and a consistent rhythm.

### The proxy gradient (Kind B - the entraining light signal itself degrades and is finally inverted)

- **FULL:** an outdoor light-dark cycle with dark-cued sleep. (Resolution met.)
- **ATTENUATED:** indoor daytime light with a mostly-dark, dimly-lit night. (Resolution still met.)
- **PARTIAL:** a correctly-phased clock overridden by a misaligned social schedule - social jetlag.
- **MINIMAL:** shift work and always-on connectivity, with the dark cue largely gone. Cue degraded, little resolution.
- **DEFEATED:** evening blue-light screens actively mis-setting the clock - false daylight at biological night.

This is a Kind B gradient: it is not a false cue being fired but the real entraining signal being degraded and finally inverted. All five rungs are evidenced. (Contestable points: the social-jetlag-vs-shift-work ordering, and whether screen signal-inversion is truly the floor.)

### Mismatch prediction

Electric light, blue screens, shift work, caffeine. These do not just shorten sleep; they corrupt the timing signal the whole organism synchronizes to.

### Cascade

No recorded cascade links - but functionally this clock gates metabolism, immunity, cognition, and mood, so its disruption shows up everywhere. Always screen circadian inputs in a client with diffuse, cross-domain complaints.

### What changing the inputs means

This is a near-pure inputs problem. Patching means treating the downstream mood or cognitive symptom while the clock stays mis-set. Changing the inputs means restoring the light-dark signal: bright daylight, dark-cued sleep onset, consistent timing, and removing the false daylight of evening screens.

### Evidence

20 evidence links. Key researchers: Walker, Roenneberg, Czeisler. This mechanism is **forced** by convergences C3, C9, and C12.

## M8 - Immune Regulation

### What it looks like in a person

This is the cytokine signaling system that produces sickness behavior - and crucially, **it overrides voluntary intention.** It is also the hub connecting inflammation to depression via cross-system cascade. In the room: fatigue, withdrawal, low mood, anhedonia, "I just can't" - the same profile as depression, because inflammation produces it. A client cannot willpower their way out of cytokine-driven sickness behavior.

What resolves it: pathogen cleared, inflammation resolved.

### The proxy gradient (Kind B - the trigger drifts from a clearable pathogen toward psychosocial/lifestyle signals with nothing to clear, and the off-switch degrades)

- **FULL:** acute infection or injury, fought off and fully resolved.
- **ATTENUATED:** recurrent acute stress that resolves each time but with incomplete recovery.
- **PARTIAL:** sustained lifestyle drivers - sedentarism, poor diet, sleep loss - keeping inflammation simmering.
- **MINIMAL:** chronic social isolation and status stress defeating the inflammatory off-switch. Cue fires, nothing to clear.
- **DEFEATED:** self-sustaining chronic low-grade inflammation, allostatic-load collapse.

All five rungs are evidenced. (The lifestyle-drivers-over-social-isolation ordering is contestable and could legitimately swap or collapse.)

### Mismatch prediction

Chronic low-grade inflammation from diet, sedentarism, and social isolation. The system was built to fire at a pathogen and shut off when it cleared; modern drivers keep it firing with nothing to clear.

### Cascade

No recorded cascade links, but this is described as the **hub** connecting inflammation to depression across the wider system. When you see a depression-like picture, inflammation is a candidate input - and the relevant inputs (diet, movement, sleep, social connection) sit in M13, M10, M7, and M3.

### What changing the inputs means

Patching means treating the resulting low mood as a primary mood disorder while the inflammatory drivers continue. Changing the inputs means addressing the lifestyle and social signals - diet, sedentarism, sleep, isolation, status stress - that keep the off-switch from engaging. Note that this is partly reversible by environmental correction.

### Evidence

37 evidence links. Key researchers: Dantzer, Danese, Irwin. This mechanism is **forced** by convergences C3, C7, and C9.

## M9 - Care / Alloparenting

### What it looks like in a person

The parental and alloparental care system, running on oxytocin and prolactin circuits. The defining claim: **the organism expects a village, not solo parenting.** In the room this is the burned-out parent - the client who loves their child and is nonetheless depleted past function, often blaming themselves. The framework reframes that: the architecture expected distributed care that is not being supplied.

What resolves it: a distributed care network rather than solo or dyadic parenting. Traditional-society data typically shows multiple non-parent caregivers (about 4 or more). The critical resolving variable is the presence and continuity of **real alloparental supply** - care plus provisioning - not bare kinship.

### Two separate scales

This mechanism has two distinct axes. They must be read separately, never merged.

**Axis A - real alloparental supply** (a deprivation axis, ordered by caregiver count times continuity, not kinship):

- **FULL:** a distributed network of about 4 or more kin and community caregivers.
- **REDUCED:** multiple reliable caregivers, including high-quality paid care. (Resolution still met.)
- **THIN:** a single intermittent helper, or high-turnover institutional care. (Partly resolved.)
- **EMPTY SHELF:** a solo or dyadic parent with no network. This is the deprivation floor - an absence of input, not a proxy.

**Axis B - fake-fix proxy** (real alloparental function is essentially zero, ordered by how completely the caregiver-other is counterfeited):

- **ZERO:** a screen or app babysitter, escalating to an AI or robot "caregiver." (Architecture-derived.) The cue of "a caregiver is present" fires; no real alloparental function is supplied.

### Mismatch prediction

The nuclear family without an alloparental network. Parent burnout cascade. This is the empty-shelf case at scale: the input the system requires has simply been removed from the modern environment.

### What changing the inputs means

Patching means treating parental burnout as an individual stress-management or resilience deficit. Changing the inputs means building real, continuous alloparental supply - care and provisioning from multiple reliable caregivers, including high-quality paid care, which sits on the resolution side of Axis A. The thing to avoid is mistaking the Axis B counterfeit (a screen, an app, an AI "caregiver") for a fix; it fires the cue and supplies no care.

### Evidence

64 evidence links. Key researchers: Hrdy, Panksepp, Kenkel. This mechanism is **forced** by convergences C1, C4, C5, and C12.

## M10 - Movement / Regulatory

### What it looks like in a person

Physical activity functions as a zeitgeber, an anti-inflammatory, and a mood regulator. The reframe: **movement isn't just exercise - it activates repair mechanisms throughout the organism.** This is a Tier-2 (strongly supported) mechanism. In a person, deficit shows up as low mood, poor sleep timing, inflammation, and dysregulation that no amount of "exercise" fully fixes - because the system expects all-day movement, not a session.

What resolves it: movement woven into daily life rather than set aside as "exercise." Hunter-gatherers typically moved 9-15 km a day as a side effect of how they lived.

### The proxy gradient (Kind B - the real input is withdrawn, not a false cue fired; ordered by how much of the all-day muscle-activity distribution is supplied)

- **FULL:** all-day incidental movement of foraging life - locomotion plus active rest postures.
- **ATTENUATED:** a physically active job - farming, manual labor, a walking-heavy trade. (Resolution still met.)
- **PARTIAL:** a mostly sedentary day broken up by walking, standing, and fidgeting - incidental NEAT.
- **MINIMAL:** a sedentary day plus a discrete gym or exercise session - a partial re-supply, not a false cue.
- **DEFEATED:** chair-bound sedentarism with essentially no movement.

All five rungs are evidenced. (The contestable point: the gym is ranked below all-day incidental NEAT here because the axis is the movement distribution, not fitness; on a fitness or mortality axis it would swap.)

### Mismatch prediction

Sedentarism, with gym sessions as partial compensation. The framework is explicit that a workout is a partial re-supply of a withdrawn input - useful, but not the same as the all-day distribution the system expects.

### Cascade

M10 is suppressed by threat (M1) and bonding's grief state (M3) - so a sedentary, withdrawn client may be downstream of threat or grief, not "unmotivated."

### What changing the inputs means

Patching means prescribing "exercise" as a standalone behavior. Changing the inputs means restoring incidental, all-day movement to how the person lives - and recognizing that a discrete gym session, while genuinely beneficial, is a MINIMAL-rung partial re-supply, not full resolution.

### Evidence

29 evidence links. Key researchers: Pontzer, Lieberman, Ratey. This mechanism is **strongly supported**, forced by convergences C3 and C9.

## M11 - Cooperation / Alliance

### What it looks like in a person

Reciprocity, cheater detection, norm enforcement, and coalitional identity, combined into one system. This is Tier 2 - and the framework is honest about why: no single convergence forces a dedicated mechanism here, but four converging research streams strongly support it. In a person, deficit shows up as the corrosive sense of dealing with strangers who can defect without consequence, of belonging to no team, of effort and fairness going untracked and unrewarded.

What resolves it: a trusted reciprocal network with norm enforcement, within a group small enough for direct reputation tracking - about 150, the same Dunbar ceiling that bounds M3. Norm enforcement requires repeated direct interaction where defectors can be identified, remembered, and sanctioned. Beyond about 150, cooperation requires institutional scaffolding - formal rules, third-party enforcement, shared fictions - rather than direct reciprocity.

### The proxy gradient (cue = constant alliance/reciprocity machinery)

- **FULL:** a trusted reciprocal network under about 150, with direct reputation tracking and norm enforcement. (Evidenced.)
- **PARTIAL:** institutionally-scaffolded cooperation beyond about 150 - formal rules, third-party enforcement, shared fictions. (Evidenced.) This is resolution-via-prosthesis: institutions re-supply the same function at scale.
- **MIXED:** market exchange among strangers backed by gameable reputation systems. Proxy failure begins here.
- **MINIMAL:** coalition-signaling and parasocial alliance via symbolic in-group markers. Cue fires, no real reciprocal function reaches a real counterparty.
- **ZERO:** anonymous one-shot transactions and AI "allies" with no reputation and no shared narrative. (Architecture-derived.)

Note the unusual structure: FULL to PARTIAL is genuine resolution (institutions are a working prosthesis), so proxy failure does not begin until MIXED. (The PARTIAL/MIXED boundary - exactly where proxy failure starts - is the contestable point.)

### Mismatch prediction

Anonymous transactions, ghosting, no shared narrative. The system needs reputation that sticks and norms that are enforced; modern interaction strips both.

### Cascade

M11 is suppressed by M14 (reproduction). This is roughly 2-5 million years old (humans plus great apes).

### What changing the inputs means

Patching means treating relational cynicism or alienation as a cognitive distortion. Changing the inputs means embedding the person in a trusted reciprocal network under about 150 where reputation is tracked and defection is sanctioned - or, where scale demands it, ensuring real institutional scaffolding rather than the gameable or anonymous floor rungs.

### Evidence

73 evidence links. Key researchers: Trivers, Boehm, Fehr & Gächter, Tomasello. This mechanism is **strongly supported**, forced by convergence C11.

## M12 - Contamination Avoidance

### What it looks like in a person

Three-domain disgust - pathogen, sexual, and moral - functioning as a behavioral immune system. This is Tier 2 (strongly supported), and it carries the lowest evidence count in the atlas (12 links), so hold it more lightly than the Tier-1 mechanisms. In a person, the pathogen domain shows up as contamination fear and avoidance; the moral domain shows up as purity-coded social aversion.

What resolves it: clean food, a clean social environment, and moral disgust contained. (Note: the "moral disgust contained" clause is flagged as contested.)

### The proxy gradient (a 3-rung evidenced pathogen-disgust gradient)

- **FULL:** clean food and a clean social environment, with disgust firing on real pathogen cues.
- **PARTIAL:** vulnerability-calibrated over-firing - for example in pregnancy or immunosuppression. Partly resolved; the system raises its threshold appropriately.
- **MIXED:** behavioral-immune smoke-detector false positives on harmless people. This is the floor.

### Demoted: the moral-disgust extension

Separately, and explicitly **not a rung on the ladder**, there is a speculative and contested moral-disgust extension: purity-coded social aversion plus weaponized moral disgust and purity spirals. This is unordered and rests on the weakest evidence (confirmed by Tybur that moral disgust is functionally distinct from pathogen disgust, sharing only the expressive component, with a near-zero effect size in one source). The purity-spiral and dehumanization mismatch prediction lives entirely here, on this weakest evidence. Treat any clinical use of it with corresponding caution.

### Mismatch prediction

Moral disgust weaponized in politics, purity spirals. Again - this prediction sits on the demoted, weakest-evidence branch.

### What changing the inputs means

For the well-supported pathogen domain, patching means treating contamination over-firing as a standalone obsessive symptom while ignoring that the system is a false-positive-biased smoke detector by design. Changing the inputs means clean food and a clean social environment, and recognizing the smoke-detector false positives on harmless people for what they are. For the moral domain, the framework's caution is itself the clinical guidance: the evidence is thin.

### Evidence

12 evidence links. Key researchers: Rozin, Curtis, Schaller. This mechanism is **strongly supported**, forced by convergences C2 and C8.

## M13 - Energy Regulation

### What it looks like in a person

Hunger, satiety, and constrained total energy expenditure, framed through Pontzer's constrained energy model - the insight that energy is **redistributed, not simply burned.** In a person, the visible problem is intake that does not stop when it should: the client who eats and is not satisfied, who craves engineered food, whose satiety signal seems broken. The framework's claim is that it is not broken - it is being defeated.

What resolves it: effortful, diverse, seasonal whole foods, and metabolic variety.

### The proxy gradient (Kind B - the satiety/intake-termination signal is progressively disabled then actively defeated; scale = food-processing intensity)

- **FULL:** effortful, diverse, seasonal whole foods. (Evidenced.)
- **ATTENUATED:** minimally processed traditional whole foods. (Resolution still met.)
- **PARTIAL:** conventionally processed, energy-dense food.
- **MINIMAL:** energy-dense palatable food optimized for crave. Cue fires, satiety begins to fail.
- **DEFEATED:** ultra-processed bliss-point food, an engineered supernormal stimulus. (Evidenced.) The off-switch is actively defeated.

The endpoints are evidenced; the middle three rungs are architecture-derived. (The contestable point: whether the ATTENUATED/PARTIAL/MINIMAL middle is three real tiers or just a bimodal whole-versus-ultra-processed split.)

### Mismatch prediction

Ultra-processed foods defeating satiety signals. This is the engineered supernormal stimulus case: the food is built to fire the crave cue while disabling the signal that would say "stop."

### Cascade

No recorded cascade links - but recall that M13 is among the systems whose state can suppress pursuit (M2), and inflammation (M8) is fed by poor diet, so energy regulation is wired into the wider load picture.

### What changing the inputs means

Patching means asking a client to out-discipline a food specifically engineered to defeat their satiety signal - a losing position, because the regulatory off-switch is the thing being attacked. Changing the inputs means shifting the food environment toward effortful, diverse, seasonal whole foods, where the termination signal can actually function.

### Evidence

34 evidence links. Key researchers: Pontzer, Keys. This mechanism is **strongly supported**, forced by convergences C3, C6, and C9.

## M14 - Reproductive Motivation

### What it looks like in a person

Built on Fisher's three-system model - desire, attraction, and pair bonding - which can each operate independently of one another. This system is independent from general pursuit (M2) and runs partly on the HPG axis, not only on brain circuits. The framework notes it shapes far more of human behavior than reproduction itself. In a person, deficit and hijack show up as compulsive use of sexual or romantic proxies alongside an absence of real pair-bonded fulfillment.

This is the one Tier-1 mechanism with a **sex-differentiated implementation** - the same system, two parameterizations:

- **Female-typical:** VMH-centered, estrous cycling, preference for resource and commitment signals, emphasis on pair-bond security.
- **Male-typical:** POA-centered, tonic testosterone, attention to fertility and accessibility cues, emphasis on sexual variety.

What resolves it: sexual fulfillment within a pair bond, plus pair-bond stability, plus reproductive success.

### The proxy gradient

- **FULL:** a real partner.
- **PARTIAL:** an attenuated real relationship, e.g. a long-distance relationship. Real bond, reduced presence.
- **MIXED:** a partial proxy - a dating app that leads to a real meeting.
- **MINIMAL:** a pure soft proxy - romance fiction, parasocial attachment. Cue fires, no real resolution.
- **ZERO:** a pure hard proxy - pornography, AI sexual products. Cue fires; nothing in the resolution conditions is supplied.

### Mismatch prediction

Pornography, dating apps, delayed reproduction, and social-media mate-value comparison. These fire desire, attraction, or bonding cues without supplying fulfillment within a stable pair bond.

### Cascade

M14 suppresses agency (M6) and cooperation (M11), and is itself suppressed by threat (M1), immune activation (M8), and care (M9). It interacts with bonding (M3), status (M5), and pursuit (M2). Clinically: high threat, inflammation, or active caregiving load can each suppress reproductive motivation - so low desire may be a downstream signal, not a primary problem.

### What changing the inputs means

Patching means treating compulsive proxy use as a standalone behavioral addiction in isolation. Changing the inputs means addressing what is suppressing or failing to resolve the system - threat, inflammation, the absence of a stable pair bond - and recognizing that the hard proxies at the floor fire the cue maximally while supplying none of the pair-bond stability and fulfillment that resolve it.

### Evidence

58 evidence links. Key researchers: Panksepp, Fisher, Buss, Trivers, Hrdy. This mechanism is **forced** by convergence C14.

## R1 - Touch

### What it looks like in a person

R1 is **not a motivational system.** It is a regulatory input to social bonding (M3), carried by C-tactile afferents. Treat it as a channel that feeds M3, not as a drive in its own right. This is Tier 3 (moderate evidence), and the framework flags it as contested - the premise that its cue can be separated from its function is itself disputed. In a person, the deficit is touch starvation contributing to the dysregulation you would otherwise attribute purely to M3.

What resolves it: regular physical contact with trusted others.

### The proxy gradient (contested; ordered by remaining relational/bond function)

- **FULL:** affectionate touch from a trusted, reciprocating other.
- **PARTIAL:** affective touch from a non-intimate caregiver, or massage.
- **MIXED:** incidental, non-affective everyday contact.
- **MINIMAL:** a weighted or deep-pressure substitute, no living other. (Speculative/future-conditional.)
- **ZERO:** synthetic, haptic, or AI C-tactile stimulation, with zero relational embedding. (Speculative/future-conditional.)

Note this gradient is not strictly monotonic: MINIMAL and ZERO both have zero relational function and are split only by a secondary cue-magnitude axis. The two floor rungs are explicitly speculative.

### Mismatch prediction

Touch-starved modern environments. The most striking evidence here is the kangaroo-care finding: roughly a 40% reduction in mortality in premature infants given skin contact - a vivid illustration that physical touch is a load-bearing regulatory input, not a luxury.

### What changing the inputs means

Because R1 feeds M3, patching the person is largely beside the point - this is an input channel. Changing the inputs means restoring regular affectionate physical contact with trusted, reciprocating others. The speculative floor rungs (weighted substitutes, synthetic haptics) supply some cue magnitude but, by definition, zero relational embedding - so they cannot substitute for the bonded contact at the top of the ladder, and the framework holds them with appropriate caution.

### Evidence

18 evidence links. Key researchers: McGlone, Field. This mechanism is graded **moderate** and is forced by convergences C4 and C5.
