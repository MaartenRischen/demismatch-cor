## The Gap - what your body expects vs what it gets

Your brain and body were built for a world that doesn't really exist anymore - small, familiar groups, real daylight and dark nights, lots of moving around, food you had to work for, people physically close to you. A lot of what feels off today isn't you being broken. It's the gap between the world you're wired for and the world you're in.

A few of the big ones (these are best-guess patterns from studying groups still living that way - real, but not exact rules):

- **People you actually know.** You're built to track a few close people and a wider circle of maybe ~150 you genuinely know - not thousands of followers and a feed full of strangers.
- **Light and sleep.** Your inner clock is set by real daylight and real darkness. Bright screens at night shove that clock later. The problem is usually the *timing* of your sleep, not just the hours.
- **Moving.** Bodies expect all-day low-key movement, not sitting for hours then one workout.
- **Food.** You're wired for food that took effort to get, not endless engineered snacks that hit the craving without the payoff.
- **Touch and closeness.** Being physically near trusted people calms your body. We've quietly removed a lot of that.

The trap in every case: the *craving* still fires exactly the same. A fake version can set off the urge without giving your body what the urge was actually for.
