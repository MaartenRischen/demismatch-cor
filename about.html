About.

I'm Maarten Rischen. I built Cor v0 over the past year, full-time, out of Pai, Thailand. This page is for anyone who wants to check there's a person behind the work before reading the atlas.

What I'm building

Cor is an atlas of the human motivational-emotional architecture. What each evolved need is, what mechanism produces its signal, what the signal is for, and what stops it once it's met. Every claim traces to primary literature.

v0 is the prototype. v1 is the rebuild that makes it academically rigorous. I direct. The 12-month plan pairs me with one academic hire at postdoc level in evolutionary psychiatry or behavioral ecology, who carries the domain rigor and builds v1.

How I got here

My background is music. MA from Royal University Groningen, residencies at Banff Centre (2015) and Trinity College Dublin (2016-2017). In 2024 I did a musical theatre production called Most Human Post-Human at Adelaide Fringe with director Gavin Robins (King Kong on Broadway).

That production is where Cor took its current shape. The question I was writing music around - what happens when exponential technology meets an organism that hasn't changed in 200,000 years? - stopped working as an artistic prompt and started demanding an evidence base. After Adelaide I went full-time on building one.

I came at this sideways. I'm not a domain academic. The plan accounts for that: the academic hire and external review carry the rigor, not me.

What v0 is

What v0 is.

v0 is a proof of concept. It shows the format is possible to build, with one mechanism (M3, attachment-based co-regulation) operationalized to coding-criteria level. It is not academically rigorous yet. v1 is the rebuild that gets it there.

The v1 researcher is not being hired to ratify v0. They are being hired to rebuild it rigorously, which includes changing, downgrading, or deleting any part that does not survive stronger review.

v1

The authoritative Cor gets built ground-up by an academic researcher at postdoc level or equivalent, in evolutionary psychiatry, behavioral ecology, or an adjacent field. Primary responsibility for evidence quality and methodological rigor rests with them.

v1 is what the SFF Main Track application funds. I orchestrate. They build.

Is this made by AI

The intellectual direction is mine. Thesis, mechanism architecture, foundational thinker selection, derivation chain. For the build I used a multi-agent AI research pipeline for database work, code, and literature processing at scale.

That pipeline is also why v0 has data-quality problems. It produced extractions and links I didn't always catch in review. v1 uses AI too. The difference is that the person running it will have the domain expertise to catch what I didn't.

Why publish v0 at all

Because the hardest thing to prove about a new kind of research object is that it can exist. v0 proves that. The structure, the derivation chain, the mechanism architecture, the atlas format - those are the contribution. The data is what needs proper execution, and that's what the grant funds.

The numbers

What the canonical counts mean.

The public stats panel reports ten numbers. Here's what lives inside each, and which carry honest caveats. As of 14 April 2026.

573 Extractions

The extraction total adds up four kinds, not only the primary ones:

  • 487 primary - direct claims from pillar and key works
  • 77 propagation - downstream claims forced by an already-extracted primary
  • 5 challenge - claims that contest or qualify an extraction elsewhere
  • 4 empirical - demonstrations whose evidential structure is itself an extraction

Earlier surfaces reported "487 Primary extractions." That number is still correct for the primary layer alone. I report 573 on the canonical panel so the primary layer doesn't look like the whole evidence base.

14 Gaps - two kinds

The gap inventory splits into two categories with different remediation paths.

  • G1-G10 · Framework-level - holes in the specification itself. Questions the atlas cannot answer because the primary literature hasn't converged, or because the evolutionary explanation is under-specified. These are the questions v1's academic program is organized around.
  • G11-G14 · Corpus-engineering - known coverage limits in the works selection, ingestion pipeline, or extraction process. They don't require new research. They require the v1 rebuild under proper methodology.

The public atlas gap section shows all 14 grouped by category.

15 Mechanisms (14 + R1 Touch)

The mechanism inventory is M1-M14 plus R1 Touch. R1 is a Tier 3 regulatory input to M3 Social Bonding (C-tactile afferents), not a standalone motivational system. I count it in the panel and keep the "(14 + R1 Touch)" parenthetical so a reader sees the total without losing the distinction. Tier breakdown: 10 Tier-1 (forced), 4 Tier-2 (strongly supported), 1 Tier-3 (moderate).

17 Foundations - by layer

The foundation stack has four layers: 2 frames (OF1 Fitness Interface, OF2 Signal-Default Epistemology), 3 premises (P1 Inclusive Fitness, P2 Domain-Sensitive Interacting Adaptations, P3 Systematic Mismatch), 9 properties (DA1-DA9), 3 consequences (DC1-DC3).

101 Works - by importance

The bibliography holds 59 pillar works (load-bearing for a specific foundation or mechanism), 39 key works (strong empirical or synthetic contributions), and 3 supporting works (historical precedents and adjuncts).

63 Researchers - by tier

The researcher list holds 34 foundational (primary spec contributors), 21 empirical (evidence-layer), and 8 adjacent (complementary frameworks and benchmarks).

864 extraction × mechanism links

The density number, surfaced separately on the cor home page. The 573 extractions collectively carry 864 mechanism-linking edges. On average, each linked extraction grounds about 1.66 mechanisms. I keep this separate from the headline extraction count so the two can't be mistaken for inconsistent versions of the same thing.
