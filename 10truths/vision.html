AI alignment

Preference-based alignment inherits whatever contaminates the preference signal. The field already knows the readout can be corrupted - reward hacking, preference misspecification, and sycophancy are all active work on exactly that. What that work does not settle is what the preference is a readout of. A preference can be ancient machinery firing in an environment it was never built for. Align to the readout without a model of the machinery and you do not align AI with humans; you align it with the mismatch, and you industrialize it. We already ran that experiment at civilizational scale. It is called the attention economy. Cor is a candidate account of the machinery.

The alternative isn't better inference. It's specification. Align the AI being built on us to what humans need, beneath what they say they want.

Aligned AI as builder. The machines that build environments at scale will be AI. Point them at preferences and you get perfect proxies. Point them at what people actually need and you get the real thing. Same machine, opposite result. Alignment and world-building are one project.

Medium is not the constraint. Resolution conditions are. A VR headset that gives you real presence, people who know you, actual stakes, and work you share is a real match. More of one than most suburbs. And it ships in years, where a city takes generations.

Scale ≈ Dunbar. Identity, dependence, governance: keep them at the size the brain evolved to track. A nation is plumbing for coordinating millions. It was never somewhere you could belong.

Role, not job. A role is something the group can see. It matters to whether they make it, and they'd feel it if you left. A job is an interchangeable input sold into a market that never learns your name. We're built for the first one.

Purpose as infrastructure. Waking up needed by specific, named people, for work that matters. You don't catch that up on a weekend or stumble into it at forty. You have it daily, or you feel the hole where it should be. Most of what we call a meaning crisis is that hole.

Dynamic egalitarianism. The only status that sits right with the organism is the kind you earn inside a group that can see you, and lose the moment you stop earning it. Rank that answers to what you actually do, and that the group can take back.

Economic substrate. A basic income, or something like it, is not charity. It's the floor everything else stands on. Stop forcing people to sell their hours into anonymous markets just to earn the right to exist, and most of the rest opens up.

Sovereign groups. Give small, Dunbar-scale groups real legal standing, as the layer where belonging and governance actually live. Nobody's seceding. The unit of belonging just moves down to a size a person can hold.
