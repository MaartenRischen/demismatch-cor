What I'm building

Cor is an evidence-graded atlas of the human motivational-emotional architecture, built toward a specification. What each evolved need is, what mechanism produces its signal, what the signal is for, and what stops it once it's met. Every evidential claim carries explicit provenance: a DOI or a PMID and a verbatim quote. Where provenance is incomplete or quarantined, the atlas says so.

The atlas is what stands today. The next phase of work is the reliability and evidence pass that makes it independently applicable. I direct. The 12-month plan pairs me with one academic hire at postdoc level in evolutionary psychiatry or behavioral ecology, who carries the domain rigor and leads that work.

How I got here

My background is music. MA from Royal University Groningen, residencies at Banff Centre (2016) and Trinity College Dublin (2017). In 2024 I did a musical theatre production called Most Human Post-Human at Adelaide Fringe with director Gavin Robins (King Kong on Broadway).

That production is where Cor took its current shape. The question I was writing music around - what happens when exponential technology meets an organism that hasn't changed in 200,000 years? - stopped working as an artistic prompt and started demanding an evidence base. After Adelaide I went full-time on building one.

I came at this sideways. I'm not a domain academic. The plan accounts for that: the academic hire and external review carry the rigor, not me.

What this is

What this is.

The atlas covers 15 motivational mechanisms and 17 foundations, built on graded extractions from primary works, structured as a derivation chain. It runs from two operating frames through three premises, nine design affordances, and three design consequences, into the 15 mechanisms and 14 convergences between them. M3 (attachment-based co-regulation) is operationalized to coding-criteria level as the worked example.

Each step is a move a reader can check, and each evidential claim carries explicit provenance. A spec of the human motivational-emotional architecture does not exist until someone builds one. This is the first pass at what one looks like.

How it was built

Sources are books from my personal library and papers harvested via PubMed and bibliographic chains. Full-text access for journals I couldn't reach came through Groningen's university library, courtesy of Han-Thomas Adriaenssen. All primary sources. No textbooks, no secondary summaries.

Three rules ran the build. Every work carries a DOI or PMID, or sits in physical collection and is marked as such. Every extraction draws from full text; no snippet-only sourcing. Every author quote is verbatim, not stitched or paraphrased into one.

Grading runs at three layers. Works are tiered pillar, key, or supporting by how load-bearing each one is. Foundations carry epistemic grades that distinguish convergent evidence from single-source claims. Mechanisms carry tier ratings for evidential strength: 10 Tier 1, 4 Tier 2, 1 Tier 3.

The extraction and linking ran through a multi-agent AI pipeline. I set the spec, chose the foundations and mechanisms, wrote the derivation chain, and ran review on what came out. The pipeline did corpus work at scale, which is how the atlas reached this coverage after a year of full-time work. It is also the source of the data-quality problems the next phase of work fixes. Extractions found to be snippet-only sourced are quarantined and held out of the evidence base. That is the discipline in practice: audit catches things and the quarantine holds them out.

What ships now, and what comes next

The next phase of work is led by a postdoc in evolutionary psychiatry or behavioral ecology. They are not hired to ratify what stands. They are hired to re-audit it, with full latitude to change, downgrade, or delete any part that does not survive stronger review. I orchestrate. They build. That is what the SFF Main Track funds.

Cor gets published now because the hardest thing to prove about a new kind of research object is that the object can exist and be inspected. The atlas shows it can. The format is the contribution: the derivation chain from OF1 and OF2 through the foundations and convergences to M3 as the worked mechanism. That is what the next phase of work inherits. The data is what it redoes under domain-expert methodology.

The numbers

What the canonical counts mean.

The atlas reports its counts live on the Cor home page. Here is what lives inside each category, and which carry caveats. For current values, read the live counts.

Extractions

The extraction total adds up four kinds, not only the primary ones:

  • Primary - direct claims from pillar and key works
  • Propagation - downstream claims forced by an already-extracted primary
  • Challenge - claims that contest or qualify an extraction elsewhere
  • Empirical - demonstrations whose evidential structure is itself an extraction

Earlier versions reported only the primary layer. The canonical panel reports all four kinds together so the primary layer doesn't look like the whole evidence base.

Gaps - two kinds

The gap inventory splits into two categories with different remediation paths.

  • Framework-level - holes in the specification itself. Questions the spec cannot answer because the primary literature hasn't converged, or because the evolutionary explanation is under-specified. These are the questions the academic program is organized around.
  • Corpus-engineering - known coverage limits in the works selection, ingestion pipeline, or extraction process. They don't require new research. They require the next phase of work under proper methodology.

The public spec gap section shows the current gaps grouped by category.

15 Mechanisms (14 + R1 Touch)

The mechanism inventory is M1-M14 plus R1 Touch. R1 is a Tier 3 regulatory input to M3 Social Bonding (C-tactile afferents), not a standalone motivational system. I count it in the panel and keep the "(14 + R1 Touch)" parenthetical so a reader sees the total without losing the distinction. Tier breakdown: 10 Tier-1 (forced), 4 Tier-2 (strongly supported), 1 Tier-3 (moderate).

17 Foundations - by layer

The foundation stack has four layers: 2 frames (OF1 Fitness Interface, OF2 Signal-Default Epistemology), 3 premises (P1 Inclusive Fitness, P2 Domain-Sensitive Interacting Adaptations, P3 Systematic Mismatch), 9 properties (DA1-DA9), 3 consequences (DC1-DC3).

Works - by importance

The bibliography tiers works as pillar (load-bearing for a specific foundation or mechanism), key (strong empirical or synthetic contributions), and supporting (historical precedents and adjuncts). Current counts are on the Cor home page.

Researchers - by tier

The researcher list sorts into foundational (primary spec contributors), empirical (evidence-layer), and adjacent (complementary frameworks and benchmarks). Current counts are on the Cor home page.

Extraction × mechanism links

The density number, shown separately on the Cor home page. Each linked extraction grounds one or more mechanisms, so the edge count runs well above the extraction count. I keep this separate from the headline extraction count so the two can't be mistaken for inconsistent versions of the same thing. Current counts are on the Cor home page.
