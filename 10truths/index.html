Aligned AI as builder. The machines that design and run environments at scale will be AI. Aligned to preferences, they build perfect proxies. Aligned to the spec, they build environments that match. The two tracks are one project.

Medium is not the constraint. Resolution conditions are. A VR/AR environment that delivers presence, named others, real stakes, and shared work is a real match. Cities take generations; matched environments in software ship in years.

Scale ≈ Dunbar. Primary identity, dependence, and governance at the size the brain evolved to track. The nation-state is coordination infrastructure - never a habitable identity unit.

Role, not job. A role is legible to the group, tied to its flourishing, irreplaceable in the short term. A job is an interchangeable input sold into an anonymous market. Humans are specified for roles.

Purpose as infrastructure. Waking up needed by specific named people for consequential work - not recovered on weekends, not discovered at forty. The meaning crisis is a mismatch readout.

Dynamic egalitarianism. Status the organism reads as legitimate: prestige-based, earned in-group, reversible, visible. Hierarchy that responds to contribution and can be withdrawn.

Economic substrate. UBI or equivalent - not welfare, the precondition. Remove the need to sell time into anonymous markets to afford to exist, and the rest becomes possible.

Sovereign groups. Legal recognition of Dunbar-scale units as the substantive social and governance layer. Not secession - moving the unit of belonging to the scale the organism is specified for.

Augment from the baseline. Once environments match, technology extends flourishing instead of displacing it. Demismatch the world - then augment.

Frequently asked about Demismatch
What is Demismatch?

Demismatch is the whole project. It starts from a single claim: modern human distress is, in most cases, accurate signaling about a world that no longer fits the people in it, not a disorder inside those people. The systems that produce human wanting, fear, and feeling are working. The environment they are running in is wrong. Demismatch sets out to specify what humans are actually built to need, and then to build to it.

So what is Cor, and how is it different from Demismatch?

Cor is the central reference work inside Demismatch. It is the spine, not the whole animal. Demismatch is the project; Cor is the atlas at the center of it that describes what a human being is at the level of evolved mechanism. Everything else in the project is built on top of Cor.

How is the project structured?

Four parts. There is the Why - the case that the problem is mismatch, that suffering is signal, that the fix is the world rather than the person. There is Cor, the source - the evidence-graded account of what humans need and how those needs operate. Then two arms built on Cor: one pointing at machines (aligning AI to the specification of what humans are), one pointing at people (tools that let a person see the mismatch they are in). And there is the Horizon - what comes after, when environments are built to fit human biology rather than against it.

Why does a project about human suffering care so much about AI?

Because the machines that will design, coordinate, and operate human environments at scale are being built right now against an unexamined assumption: that what a person clicks, says, or prefers is reliable evidence of what is good for them. AI aligned to current preferences builds perfect proxies and deepens the mismatch. AI aligned to the specification of what humans actually need can build environments that match. The alignment work and the environment work are not two projects. They are the same project.

The two arms
What is the machine-facing arm?

Alignment to Cor as ground truth - giving machines something to read about what humans are, underneath the layer of what humans say they want. Three lines: a machine-readable specification any model can query live; correction through contrastive pairs drawn from named mechanisms; and models built on the specification from the start, aimed at the places where hallucinating about human needs is most costly - clinical triage, educational scaffolding, therapy-adjacent tools.

What is the human-facing arm?

Software that shows a person what their environment is doing to their biology. Two families: Decode reads the mismatch - you point it at something and it returns the mechanism firing, the proxy modern life is using to hit it, and the real input that would resolve it. Adaptor builds the matched conditions back - tools that help put the missing inputs in front of you, starting with Lens, a morning tool that puts a role, a group, and a goal that matters back in your field of view.

Which of these tools actually exist right now?

Decode Talk is live - the chat version, where you type a situation and get the read back. Decode Web (a browser extension that annotates pages) and Decode Camera (which reads a photo of a scene) are in build. Adaptor, starting with Lens, is next. There are openable, working public tools today.

What does Decode give me that a wellness app doesn't?

A wellness app tells you to feel better. An AI companion sits in the place of a relationship and returns none of the thing a relationship delivers - the most dangerous proxy of all, because it fires the bonding response while supplying nothing that resolves it. Decode does neither. It names what is happening: which evolved system is sounding, what cheap modern stand-in is setting it off, and what the real thing would be. The aim is to make the mismatch legible so you can act on the world.

Why you feel so bad
Why do you feel so bad?

Because you are a finely working organism in a world that does not fit you. The systems that produce your moods evolved to run in a small band of known people, under the sun, doing work that mattered to those people. You are running them in a world of strangers, screens, and anonymous markets. The bad feeling is those systems reporting the gap accurately. It is not malfunction. It is a correct readout of a wrong environment.

Is something wrong with you, or with your environment?

Your environment. This is the whole point, and it does not soften with repetition. The hardware works. Nothing in you is broken or defective or deficient. You have been handed conditions no human was built to run on, and your body is telling you so in the only language it has. The fault was never located in you. It is the gap, and the fact that the gap has been made to look normal.

Why can't you stop ruminating?

Because your problem-solving system is built to keep working a threat until it is resolved, and most modern threats never resolve. A predator leaves or it doesn't. A debt, a status hit, a relationship that went wrong, a future you can't control - these have no clean end state, so the loop never gets the signal to close. It is not weakness of will. It is a closing mechanism with nothing to close on. The loop stays open because the input stays open.

What is anxiety actually?

A threat detector doing its job in a world full of cues it cannot switch off. The system that scans for danger evolved to err toward false alarms, because historically the cost of missing a real threat was death and the cost of a false alarm was a wasted sprint. So it is loud and it is biased toward "danger" on purpose. Modern life feeds it an endless stream of things that look like threats - the inbox, the news, the crowd of strangers, the bill - none of which it can resolve by fighting or fleeing. Anxiety is that detector firing correctly at inputs it was never built to face.

What is depression actually?

In this framework, low mood is often a system pulling you back when something is wrong and effort isn't paying off. It can be a signal to stop, to conserve, to withdraw from a pursuit that is not working or a situation you cannot win. That signal becomes a trap when the underlying condition never changes - no role, no group that needs you, no work that matters, no daylight, no movement, no people. Then the pull-back never lifts, because the thing it was telling you to escape is still there every morning. The mood is reading the conditions. Change the conditions and the reading changes. (This is a framework account of a signal, not a clinical diagnosis - if you are in a dark place, see a real human professional. The two are not in conflict.)

Why does none of the usual advice fix it?

Because almost all of it tries to fix you - your thoughts, your habits, your attitude - while leaving the conditions untouched. You can get better at managing a signal, and the signal will keep coming, because it is accurate. Telling a smoke detector to calm down does nothing about the smoke. The interventions that actually move the needle change the input: real people close by, a role that matters, daylight, movement, work tied to specific named others. The advice fails because it is aimed at the wrong layer.

So what do you actually do about it?

Stop looking for the defect in yourself, because there isn't one, and start reading your environment. Which of your systems is firing. What cheap modern stand-in is setting it off. What the real input would be. That is what the Decode tools are for - to make the mismatch legible so you can act on the world instead of managing your reaction to it. The fix is never "be different." It is "build conditions that fit what you already are."

The horizon
What is the project actually building toward?

Environments that fit human biology. Concretely: people waking up inside conditions matched to what they are built for - a role the group depends on, a group at roughly the scale the human brain evolved to track, work that matters to specific named people, and a basis for living that does not require selling time into an anonymous market just to afford to exist. The claim is that when the environment is right, much of what now reads as individual pathology dissolves, because it was a readout of the gap all along.

Isn't this just nostalgia for tribal life? Are you telling people to go live in the woods?

No. The ancestral environment is used as a measuring instrument, not a destination. What matters is the function a system is built to receive, never its ancient form. A bonding system needs the function of trusted people close by; it does not need you to live in a hut. An environment built in software that delivers embodied presence, named others, real stakes, and shared work is a real match. Matched environments in software can ship in years.

Where does technology fit in all this? Is the project anti-tech?

No. Technology is neutral - what matters is whether it delivers the function a mechanism is checking for. The problem is not the tools. It is aiming them at preferences instead of needs, inside an environment that is already mismatched, where every improvement just makes the proxy smoother while the signal stays unmet.

What is "demismatch first, then augment"?

It is the test the whole project applies. Before adding any new capability - to a person, a tool, an environment - ask whether the underlying environment matches what humans need. If it does not, augmentation deepens the mismatch rather than helping. Fix the fit first, then augment from that baseline. It is the sentence the whole horizon is organized around.

Standing back
Who is this for?

Several audiences. People trying to understand their own distress without being told the fault is in them. AI labs and alignment researchers who need a model of the human that sits underneath stated preference. Clinicians, educators, designers, and policy people who work with human environments. And funders and researchers interested in foundational infrastructure for understanding what a human being is.

Is this proprietary? Can other people build on it?

The atlas at the center is built as open foundational infrastructure, on purpose. It describes what a human is at the level of evolved mechanism, and any team can build on that - in technology, policy, education, clinical practice, or design. The alignment work is one application. The human-facing tools are another. Neither is the limit of what the source is for.

Where do I start?

Start with the Why if you want the case for why the problem is mismatch. Start with Cor if you want to see the evidence and the structure underneath everything. Try Decode Talk if you want to point the lens at something in your own life. And read the Horizon if you want to know what the whole thing is being built toward.

Frequently asked about Cor
What it is
What is Cor, in one sentence?

Cor is an evidence-graded atlas of the complete human motivational and emotional architecture: a map of the systems that drive what humans want, feel, fear, and pursue, and what each of those systems was built to need.

What does "atlas" mean here? I've also seen the word "specification."

Cor is currently an atlas. It maps the territory. The specification is the operational layer it is being built toward, and that word is reserved for that future layer. The precedent is the Allen Brain Atlas or the Human Cell Atlas: open foundational infrastructure that describes what a thing is, built so that anyone can build on top of it.

What is the core idea behind it?

That modern human suffering is, in most cases, accurate signaling from a sound organism in a mismatched environment. The hardware works. The environment is wrong. Anxiety, low mood, chronic stress, loneliness, restlessness - these are usually intelligible outputs of systems doing their job, responding to conditions they were never built for. The problem is the gap between the world a human is built for and the world a human lives in. The fix is to change the world, not to locate a defect in the person.

Why does that matter beyond psychology?

Because it points at a blind spot in how we build technology, and especially how we try to align AI. The standard assumption is that what a person wants, clicks, or prefers reveals what is good for them. Cor's position is that preferences are outputs of evolved mechanisms, not ground truth. A lonely person can genuinely want an AI companion. A person caught in a feed can genuinely want the next video. The wanting is real. It is also not reliable evidence that the thing is good for them. Any system that optimizes for stated or revealed preference without a model of the architecture underneath is optimizing against the person it claims to serve. Cor is the model of that architecture. AI alignment is one application built on it. It is not the frame around it.

How it's built
How was a source chosen for inclusion in Cor?

A work has to clear three hard gates. First, it needs to be real and findable - every work needs a verifiable identifier (a DOI or PubMed ID) or must be physically in the collection. Second, anything taken from it has to come from the full text - claims sourced from an abstract, snippet, or aggregator summary are rejected outright. This rule is strict because an internal audit found a complete failure rate on claims pulled from snippets: fabricated quotes, drifted paraphrases, invented findings. Third, any direct quote stored against a work has to be word-for-word. A quote stitched from fragments counts as fabrication, not evidence.

How is the material actually turned into findings?

The process runs from the bottom up, from evidence to conclusions, never the other way around. A work is read, and individual findings are pulled from its full text. Each finding is recorded and graded for how strong the evidence is. These findings accumulate against the human systems they bear on. Where several independent lines of research - different labs, different methods, different decades - point at the same structural fact, that agreement is recorded as a convergence. The convergences are what force the systems above them into the map. A system only earns its place because the evidence underneath it converges on it. Nothing is reverse-engineered to fit a theory decided in advance.

What does the finished structure look like?

It is a pyramid, derived once and now locked. Reading from the foundation up: two framing premises about how organisms relate to the world. Then three core premises: that survival and reproduction are what evolution optimizes for, that the organism is a set of specialized interacting systems rather than one general-purpose learner, and that modern environments systematically push those systems outside the range they were built for. From those, derived properties of the architecture. Then fourteen convergences. Then, at the top, fifteen mechanisms - the actual systems that produce human motivation and emotion. This structure is final.

What are the fifteen mechanisms?

In plain terms: threat management, pursuit and exploration, social bonding, social calibration through play, status monitoring, agency, circadian regulation, immune regulation, care and alloparenting, movement, cooperation and alliance, contamination avoidance, energy regulation, reproductive motivation, and touch. Each maps to a system shaped by evolution, with a defined input the system checks for and a defined cost when that input is missing.

Quality & philosophy
What is the quality philosophy? How do you keep this honest?

One commitment organizes everything: the evidence has to earn its place, and when it cannot, it gets downgraded rather than defended. A finding is tested by whether it would survive someone actively trying to break it. When there is doubt about how strong a piece of evidence is, it is graded down, not up. The word "replicated" is reserved for genuine agreement across multiple studies. And the framework's own interpretations stay marked as theoretical unless directly tested. Two further rules: evolutionary hypotheses are never written into a system's description as established fact before they are verified; and known failures from psychology's replication crisis are flagged and excluded rather than quietly used.

Books or papers - which does Cor rely on?

Papers. Books are treated as the map; papers are the territory. A book describes research; a paper is research. So a claim traced to a popular synthesis is treated as a pointer toward the primary literature, not as the evidence itself.

Doesn't this framework just explain away anything that challenges it?

It is built specifically not to. Two conventions make that concrete, and they cut in opposite directions. When mainstream psychiatry fails - diagnostic categories that don't hold together, treatments that underperform - Cor counts that as support, because the framework predicts it. But findings from addiction neuroscience showing substance-specific neural mechanisms are treated as genuine challenges, not threats to be absorbed. The instinct to defend is exactly what is prohibited there. A framework that nothing could challenge would not be a framework.

What is the EEA, and aren't you just saying "the caveman life was better"?

No, and this distinction is load-bearing. The EEA - the environment a human's systems were shaped in - is used as a diagnostic baseline, a reference point for measuring the gap between what a system expects and what it gets. It is not a prescription, a destination, or a romanticized past. The goal is never to restore the ancestral form of anything. It is to deliver the function the system is actually checking for. A bonding system needs the function of trusted co-regulation; it does not need you to live in a hut.

"Mismatch" - does that mean evolution broke us, or that we're badly designed?

The opposite. The systems work. They are doing precisely what they were built to do. What has changed is the input. A smoke detector that goes off because the room is full of smoke is not broken. The architecture is sound; it is being fed conditions outside the range it was built for. Cor never locates the problem in the person. The villain is always the gap, and the normalization of that gap - never the human's wanting.

How does Cor fit into the larger project?

Cor is the central reference work of Demismatch. Demismatch is the project; Cor is its spine. The atlas is the shared source, and the applied work sits on top of it. For machines, that means aligning systems to the architecture as ground truth. For humans, it means two tools: reading where the mismatch is, and building environments that match. The horizon the whole thing points at is matched environments - changing the world so the deep systems stop sounding the alarm.

Yes. That is the point of building it as open foundational infrastructure rather than a proprietary product. The atlas describes what a human being is at the level of evolved mechanism. Any team - in technology, policy, education, clinical practice, or design - can build on that. AI alignment is one such application. It is not the limit of what the atlas is for.
