Methodology

What Cor v0 is.

Cor v0 is a proof of concept. It demonstrates that a formal, evidence-traced specification of the human motivational-emotional architecture is possible to build. It is not the specification itself.

What v0 demonstrates

The atlas structure works. Foundations derive into convergences, convergences force mechanisms, mechanisms predict applications. Every claim can trace down to a primary source. The derivation chain from ontological frame to mechanism-level prediction is tractable.

The 14 mechanisms (v4) and their derivational logic are the strongest part of v0. They will carry forward into v1.

What v0 is not

The data quality of the current atlas is not at a standard that would survive academic peer review. Extractions contain errors. Some sourcing is unreliable. The evidence base needs to be rebuilt from scratch by researchers with domain expertise, not cleaned up.

v0 should be read as a structural prototype, not as a citable source.

Cor v1

The authoritative Cor will be built ground-up by an academic researcher at postdoc level or equivalent, with expertise in evolutionary psychiatry, behavioral ecology, or a related field. The builder will have primary responsibility for evidence quality and methodological rigor.

v1 will be funded under the SFF Main Track grant. Maarten Rischen orchestrates; the academic hire builds.

Is this made by AI

The specification — the thesis, the mechanism architecture, the foundational thinker selection, the derivation chain, the whole intellectual direction — is human. AI tools were used to build the database, write code, and process literature at scale. Any data quality problems in v0 are partly a consequence of that process.

v1 will be built by an academic researcher with domain expertise, also using AI tools, but with the methodological rigor to catch what v0 didn't.

Why publish v0 at all

Because the hardest thing to prove about a new kind of research object is that it can exist. v0 proves that. The structure, the derivation chain, the mechanism architecture, the atlas format — these are the contribution. The data is the part that needs proper execution, and that execution is what the grant funds.

The Numbers

What the canonical counts mean.

The public stats panel reports ten numbers. This section names what is inside each, where the depth lives, and which numbers carry honest caveats.

573 Extractions

The extraction count is the total of four kinds, not only the primary ones:

  • 487 primary — direct claims from pillar and key works
  • 77 propagation — downstream claims forced by an already-extracted primary
  • 5 challenge — claims that contest or qualify an extraction elsewhere
  • 4 empirical — demonstrations whose evidential structure is itself an extraction

Earlier surfaces reported "487 Primary extractions." That number is still correct for the primary layer alone. The canonical panel reports the total (573) to avoid making the primary layer look like the whole evidence base.

13 quarantined extractions

Thirteen of the 573 extractions were originally sourced from short snippets rather than full-text works. The external Codex audit flagged a 100% failure rate on snippet-sourced extractions in the priority queue. These extractions remain in the database with a quarantine caveat rather than being deleted, so the provenance is visible and recoverable by Cor v1.

v0 does not treat quarantined extractions as load-bearing. v1 will re-derive any claim that currently rests on one from the full-text source.

14 Gaps — split by kind

The gap inventory splits into two categories with different remediation paths:

  • G1–G10 · Framework-level gaps — holes in the specification itself. Questions the atlas cannot answer because the primary literature does not yet converge, or because the evolutionary explanation is under-specified. These are the questions Cor v1's academic program is organized around.
  • G11–G14 · Corpus-engineering gaps — holes in how v0 was assembled. Known coverage limits in the works selection, ingestion pipeline, or extraction process. These do not require new research; they require the v1 rebuild under proper methodology.

The public atlas gap section shows all 14 grouped by category.

15 Mechanisms — 14 + R1 Touch

The mechanism inventory counts M1–M14 plus R1 Touch. R1 is a Tier 3 regulatory input to M3 Social Bonding (C-tactile afferents), not a standalone motivational system. The canonical panel counts it in and the "(14 + R1 Touch)" parenthetical preserves the distinction — honest about the architecture without hiding R1 from a reader who needs the total.

864 extraction × mechanism links

The density number — surfaced separately on the cor home page — reports how many mechanism-linking edges the 573 extractions collectively carry. On average, each linked extraction grounds approximately 1.66 mechanisms. The density is reported separately from the headline count so the two cannot be mistaken for inconsistent versions of the same thing.
