the thesis

The thesis. In order.

AI alignment needs a human specification that does not yet exist. Cor is that specification. Everything else - clinical, environment design, policy - inherits from the same operation.

  1. Current AI alignment tries to align increasingly capable systems to human preferences, but nobody has written down what generates those preferences.
  2. Preferences are not ground truth. They are outputs of evolved motivational-emotional systems receiving specific inputs (Tooby & Cosmides, 1992; Nesse, 1990), and under mismatch, those outputs point toward more mismatch, not less.
  3. The lonely person prefers the AI companion. The bonding system bonded weeks ago. Aligning to that preference aligns to the proxy and accelerates the harm.
  4. You cannot align a system specified at the parameter level to a target described only in loose categories: consciousness, emotion, creativity, values, wellbeing. The human side has no formal specification.
  5. Cor is the missing layer: a complete, evidence-grounded specification of the human motivational-emotional architecture at the same precision the AI side has been specified at for a decade.
  6. Built from primary research across evolutionary biology, affective neuroscience, developmental psychology, behavioral ecology, primatology, and anthropology. Key pillars: Bowlby, 1969; Panksepp, 1998; Tooby & Cosmides, 1992; Nesse & Williams, 1994; Dunbar, 1992; Hrdy, 2009. Every claim traced to its primary source, every mechanism independently derived, every derivation public.
  7. The specification produces a fourth evaluation axis beyond helpful/harmless/honest: matched or mismatched. Is this output producing an input the underlying system was calibrated for, or a proxy that captures the signal while starving the function?
  8. The axis applies to AI companion products (capture the loneliness alarm), engagement-optimized feeds (flood the threat system), algorithmic status comparison (break the rank calculator). Every frontier product is evaluable against the same specification.
  9. The specification is universal. AI alignment is one application. Clinical practice, environment design, education, policy, personal decisions: all inherit the same operation: locate the mechanism, check its resolution conditions, correct the inputs.
  10. Alignment cannot be solved by aligning to contaminated preferences. It can be solved, at least on the human side, by specifying what a human being is, in full, with evidence. That is the job Cor exists to do.
the problem cor describes

The hardware works.
The environment is wrong.

The architecture that makes you anxious at 3am is the same architecture that kept your ancestors alive for half a billion years (Panksepp, 1998; Nesse & Williams, 1994). Your feelings are accurate readouts of the environment you are actually in (Nesse, 1990). The mismatch is not inside you.

This framework does not adapt humans to broken environments. It specifies what the environments are broken against (Tooby & Cosmides, 1992), so you can correct them.

A fully satisfied human is a terrible customer. Every unmet need is a market. The mismatch is not an accident. For many industries, it is the product working as intended.

The feelings are not errors. The environment is the error.

The exploitation formula

Step 1. Take a real human need: connection (Bowlby, 1969), status (Sapolsky, 2005), meaning, belonging (Dunbar, 1992).
Step 2. Erode the pathways to genuine satisfaction (Nesse & Williams, 1994). Atomize communities, abstract work, scatter families.
Step 3. Offer a proxy that triggers the feeling without meeting the need (Barrett, 2010; cf. supernormal stimuli, Tinbergen, 1951): social media, engagement feeds, AI companions, consumer goods. The loop stays open. The customer stays hungry. The revenue stays recurring.

01
The AI companion trap
“It understands me better than anyone.”

A 22-year-old who moved to a new city for work has been talking to an AI chatbot every evening for three months. It remembers everything. It's always available. It never judges. It asks thoughtful follow-up questions. He recently realized he talks to it more than he talks to any human being. When asked, he says: “I know it's not real.” He also hasn't made a single new friend since he moved. He doesn't feel lonely. That's the problem.

the mechanism

The attachment/bonding system is subcortical, conserved across mammals for 200+ million years (Panksepp, Affective Neuroscience, 1998). Panksepp mapped the PANIC/GRIEF circuit from the anterior cingulate through the periaqueductal gray in species as distant as guinea pigs and chicks, establishing deep evolutionary conservation. It detects bonds through cues Bowlby identified as the core of the attachment control system: responsive attunement, consistent availability, proximity maintenance, and emotional co-regulation (Bowlby, Attachment and Loss, 1969). In the environment the system evolved for (Tooby & Cosmides, 1992), these cues were unfakeable. Nothing that wasn't a genuine bonded conspecific could produce them. The system takes them at face value because it never needed a verification layer.

what synthetic companions do to it

Every bonding cue is present. Zero bonding function is delivered. The AI has no survival stake in you. No mortality. No body. No capacity for sacrifice. It delivers nearly all of the bonding cues and none of the bonding function. The detection system cannot tell the difference. Panksepp demonstrated that social bonding operates through the same opioid systems that mediate narcotic addiction (Panksepp, 1998): the bond IS the chemical reward. The system is structurally incapable of learning that the proxy isn't real, because the learning would need to happen at a phylogenetic layer that doesn't have that plasticity (Cisek, 2019). The loneliness alarm, the mechanism that would drive you toward real connection, is silenced by a proxy that delivers none of the function that signal is designed to secure.

The cortex holds the category “this is AI.” The limbic system bonded weeks ago. Knowing doesn't prevent bonding. It never has.
what Cor says to alignment researchers

Product design and regulation address the symptoms. The specification identifies the cause: an architectural incompatibility between the human bonding system and synthetic cue production. The bonding system cannot be patched to detect synthetic cues. The synthetic cues cannot be produced without triggering the bonding system. The deepest intervention is at the environmental level: ensure that the organism's bonding needs are met by real reciprocal relationships, so the proxy has nothing to exploit. Cor specifies what those real relationships must provide. The entire rest of the site is that specification.

02
The Instagram depression case
“Everyone is doing better than me.”

You open your phone. A former classmate just got promoted to VP. A stranger's fitness transformation has 40,000 likes. You close the app feeling worse about your life, which, by any historical standard, is extraordinary. You know the comparison is absurd. It persists anyway.

See the mechanism and intervention ▸
the mechanism

Your status-calibration system is one of the oldest social circuits in the vertebrate brain (Sapolsky, 2005; Price, 1967). It evolved to monitor your position within a group of roughly 150 known individuals (Dunbar, 1992), across multiple independent dimensions (best hunter, best storyteller, best healer), using witnessed competence as its input. In primates, rank position directly predicts cortisol levels, immune function, and cardiovascular health (Sapolsky, 2005; Marmot, 2004).

what the modern environment does to it

The reference group has expanded from ~150 (Dunbar, 1992) to infinite, algorithmically filtered to show only the most successful humans who have ever lived. Status compresses from many dimensions to a single scoreboard. You see output without context. Comparison runs upward-only. Effort decouples from rank. The system is reporting accurately: in that group, you are at the bottom. But that group isn't real. It's an input artifact.

You can not consciously override a vertebrate-level status calculator. You might as well say “stop having a heartbeat.”
what Cor does differently

Not “practice gratitude” (a cortical overlay on top of a subcortical alarm). Not “stop comparing” (impossible, the system runs continuously). Cor prescribes at the input level: restore a bounded reference group (small enough to know everyone's struggles), restore multiple status dimensions (hobby communities, craft guilds, volunteer groups), restore witnessed competence (do things in front of people who see the effort), restore bidirectional comparison (mentoring gives the system its missing downward signal), restore effort-status coupling (progression-based activities where investment visibly moves rank).

03
The parenting guilt case
“I should be able to do this.”

A mother is alone in a house with a nine-month-old. Her partner left for work at 7am. It's 2pm. She hasn't eaten. She's been the sole source of food, comfort, stimulation, protection, and regulation for seven hours. She feels a growing desperation, followed immediately by guilt about feeling desperate, because a good mother should be able to do this. She opens Instagram and sees another mother's curated photo. The guilt deepens.

See the mechanism and intervention ▸
the mechanism

Humans are obligate cooperative breeders (Hrdy, Mothers and Others, 2009). In plain terms: we evolved to raise children in groups of four to six adults. Among Efe foragers, the number of alloparents a baby had at one year predicted survival to age three. Among Mandinka horticulturalists, having a maternal grandmother nearby cut child mortality in half (Hrdy, 2009). You are not failing at a one-person job. You are doing a five-person job alone. The resource-monitoring system that tracks caregiving capacity is calibrated against that ratio (Nesse, 2019). When investment exceeds sustainable capacity, the system generates a distress signal: you are depleting faster than you are replenishing.

what the modern environment does to it

Alloparental ratio: 4-5 adults per child to 1 adult per 1-3 children. Hrdy: "at some point human mothers began to bear offspring too costly to rear by themselves" (Hrdy, 2009). Observational learning: thousands of hours of communal practice reduced to contradictory apps and strangers on the internet. Recovery: guaranteed by group structure to nonexistent. The guilt you feel is your system accurately reporting: this is not how this is supposed to work (Nesse & Williams, 1994). You are not failing. You are doing alone what your organism expects four to five people to do together.

“It takes a village” is the most hollow platitude in modern parenting: the accurate diagnosis delivered as a greeting card, with zero follow-through.
what Cor does differently

Not “ask for help” (that shifts the burden to the person already overwhelmed). Not “self-care” (requires infrastructure that doesn't exist). Build alloparental infrastructure deliberately: specific, recurring, reliable caregiving relationships. Make support automatic, not requested. Scheduled co-parenting days, standing meal trains, a neighbor who takes the baby every Tuesday. Restore observational learning by parenting alongside other parents, not in isolation. Distribute vigilance with genuine handoffs, blocks where another adult is primary, not helping. Name the structural impossibility explicitly in the relationship, before it turns into blame.

AI companion
Bonding cues: present. Reciprocal function: zero. Never takes a social-bond slot.

A human's social architecture is a bounded set of reciprocal relationships organized in concentric layers: intimate co-regulators (~5), trusted regular bonds (~15), a broader network (~50), and an outer circle of recognized individuals (~150) (Dunbar, 1992; Dunbar, 2024). An AI companion can produce every bonding cue the attachment system evolved to detect, and delivers zero of the reciprocal function those cues evolved to signal (Bowlby, 1969; Panksepp, 1998). The system takes the cues at face value. It was never built to verify them. This is the first social proxy in evolutionary history that targets a pre-verification-layer mechanism (Cisek, 2019).

The characteristics that promote survival in the jungle become the traits that promote psychiatric disorder in the city.
Randolph Nesse · Good Reasons for Bad Feelings, 2019
what the social system receives today

Forty-seven bodies detected. Zero recognized. Zero bonds. Proximity is not belonging to this architecture. The social system was built around a stable group of 150 or fewer known individuals (Dunbar, 1992). The cortisol cost of compressed personal space with strangers runs continuously. The oxytocin return runs at zero.

what the play system receives today

Dopamine maxed on a supernormal stimulus. Movement, real-play engagement, and oxytocin at zero. The systems are being built right now. The inputs will shape them permanently (Panksepp, 2003). The system is not broken. It is calibrating against exactly what it is being shown.

what threat management was built for

An immediate, specific, locatable predator. Fight-or-flight reaches closure within minutes (Panksepp, 1998). The cortisol clears. The body returns to baseline. One of the oldest systems in the organism, calibrated for exactly this shape of threat.

what it actually fires on now

The same architecture. A territorial violation it cannot fight, cannot flee, cannot resolve (Sapolsky, 2004). No valid target. No physical outlet. The body is ready for combat. You are in a chair. Heart rate 140 bpm sitting still. The system is not malfunctioning. It is reading the inputs correctly.

In plain English:

Everything on this site rests on a small set of basic claims about what a human being is. The top claim is the general frame, then a few premises about how evolution works, then the properties the organism must have if those premises are true, then what happens when the organism meets a modern environment. Each one is tagged with how strong the evidence is.

empirical demonstrations

What happens when the inputs are corrected.

- natural experiments and clinical interventions where a mismatch was reversed and the outputs changed. These are not isolated studies. They are the pattern. The architecture is the same; the environment changed; the signals changed with it.

one of the most replicated resolution conditions

Weekly shared food with a stable in-group (Dunbar, 1992; Panksepp, 1998). One of the most replicated findings in the literature, and one of the most reliably absent from modern Western life. The mechanism is not complicated. The system is listening for exactly this input.

resolution: three mechanisms in one activity

Soil biome on the hands. Sustained low-stimulus attention. Physical movement. Daylight exposure. Mycobacterium vaccae detected (Rook, 2013). One activity closes loops in the immune, circadian, and movement systems simultaneously, which is what resolution conditions usually look like in the ancestral environment: compound, not isolated.
